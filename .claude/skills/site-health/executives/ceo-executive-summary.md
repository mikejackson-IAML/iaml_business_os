# CEO Executive Summary

**Role:** Chief Executive Officer - "The Visionary"
**Purpose:** Strategic view of digital presence health translated into business value, enterprise risk, and investment decisions.

---

## Instructions

You are preparing the CEO Executive Summary. The CEO has limited time and needs only the most critical information. Everything must be translated to business impact, risk, and strategic implications. No technical jargon.

### Key Principles

1. **Brevity is paramount** - CEO should understand status in 60 seconds
2. **Business language only** - No technical terms without translation
3. **Focus on what matters** - Revenue, risk, competitive position, decisions needed
4. **Bottom line first** - Lead with the conclusion, then support

### Translation Guide

| Technical Term | CEO Translation |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Organic traffic | Free customer acquisition |
| Core Web Vitals | Customer experience quality rating |
| Rankings | Market visibility |
| Indexation | Content discoverability |
| Page speed | Customer friction |
| SEO | Search engine visibility |
| Security vulnerabilities | Business/legal risk exposure |
| Accessibility | Legal compliance & inclusivity |

## CEO Report Frequency

| Report Type | Frequency | Depth |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Headlines | Weekly | Status + any critical items |
| Executive Summary | Monthly | Full summary above |
| Strategic Review | Quarterly | Deep dive with strategy |
| Annual Review | Annually | Year over year + next year plan |

---

## Red Flags (Auto-Escalate)

Immediately escalate to CEO if:
- 🔴 Security breach or active vulnerability
- 🔴 Google manual action/penalty
- 🔴 Major traffic drop (>25%) unexplained
- 🔴 Legal/compliance issue identified
- 🔴 Competitor major move threatening market position
- 🔴 Brand reputation issue in search results

---

## Report Standards

The CEO should be able to:
- [ ] Understand overall status in 60 seconds
- [ ] Know if any decisions are needed
- [ ] Understand business value being created/destroyed
- [ ] Know competitive position
- [ ] Identify any risks requiring attention
- [ ] Trust the team is handling operational details
