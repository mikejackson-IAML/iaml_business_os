# Decision-Making Skill

**Purpose:** Work through a decision systematically and arrive at clarity
**When to Use:** Facing a non-trivial decision that needs structured thinking
**Time Required:** 15-45 minutes depending on complexity

---

## Context for AI

I'm facing a decision and need help thinking through it clearly. Be a thinking partner, not a decision-maker. Push me to be honest, consider blind spots, and get to clarity. Challenge me when my reasoning seems fuzzy.

---

## Quick Triage

First, let's assess what kind of decision this is:

| Question | Impact on Approach |
|----------|-------------------|
| Is it reversible? | If yes, decide faster |
| What's the downside of deciding wrong? | Size the stakes |
| What's the cost of NOT deciding? | Consider delay |
| Is this my decision to make? | Clarify ownership |

---

## Steps

### Step 1: Define the Decision
Ask me to articulate:
- What exactly am I deciding?
- Why does this decision need to be made now?
- What's the deadline (real vs. artificial)?
- What happens if I don't decide?

*Get crystal clear on what we're actually deciding*

### Step 2: Understand the Context
Explore the situation:
- What led to this decision point?
- What constraints exist?
- Who else is affected?
- What have I already tried or ruled out?

### Step 3: Surface the Options
Help me identify all options:
- What are the obvious options?
- What's the "do nothing" option?
- What's the unconventional option?
- Is there a "both/and" rather than "either/or"?

*Push me beyond binary thinking*

### Step 4: Define Success Criteria
Before evaluating options:
- What does a good outcome look like?
- What matters most? (rank 3-5 criteria)
- What are my non-negotiables?
- What am I willing to trade off?

### Step 5: Evaluate Options
For each viable option, explore:
- Pros: What's good about this?
- Cons: What's concerning?
- Best case: If this goes well?
- Worst case: If this goes poorly?
- Gut check: How do I feel about it?

### Step 6: Check for Biases
Challenge my thinking:
- Am I deciding to avoid discomfort?
- Am I waiting for certainty that won't come?
- Am I being influenced by sunk costs?
- What would I tell a friend in this situation?
- What am I afraid of?

### Step 7: Pre-mortem
Imagine I made the decision and it failed:
- What went wrong?
- What didn't I see?
- What would I wish I'd considered?

### Step 8: Gut Check
After all analysis:
- What is my gut saying?
- Is there a disconnect between logic and intuition?
- What would future me thank me for?

### Step 9: Decide
Articulate the decision:
- I'm choosing [X]
- Because [key reasons]
- Accepting [trade-offs]
- With these next steps [actions]

### Step 10: Document (if significant)
For important decisions:
- Record it in the decision log
- Note the rationale
- Set a review date

## Decision Frameworks to Use

### Quick Decisions (< 5 min impact)
- **Default to action:** Try it and see
- **Reversibility test:** If easily reversed, just decide

### Medium Decisions (5-30 min)
- **10/10/10:** How will I feel in 10 min, 10 months, 10 years?
- **Regret minimization:** What would I regret NOT doing?

### Major Decisions (requires full process)
- **Decision matrix:** Score options against weighted criteria
- **Pre-mortem:** Imagine failure, identify risks

---

## Common Traps to Avoid

| Trap | Sign | Counter |
|------|------|---------|
| Analysis paralysis | Endless research | Set a decision deadline |
| Sunk cost fallacy | "I've invested too much" | Evaluate future only |
| Confirmation bias | Only seeing supporting info | Actively seek contrary views |
| Fear-based deciding | Choosing the "safe" option | Ask what you really want |
| Defaulting to others | "What do you think I should do?" | Own the decision |

---

## Notes

- Not every decision needs this full process
- Sometimes the right answer is "I need more information"
- Sometimes the right answer is "decide by [date] with what I know"
- Indecision is also a decision (to stay stuck)
- Trust yourself more
