# End of Day Closeout Skill

**Purpose:** Close out the workday intentionally, capture progress, and set up tomorrow
**When to Use:** Last 15 minutes of the workday
**Time Required:** 10-15 minutes

---

## Context for AI

I'm wrapping up my workday. Help me close out intentionally rather than just stopping mid-stream. I want to leave work "at work" mentally, feel good about what I accomplished, and set myself up for a smooth start tomorrow.

---

## Steps

### Step 1: Quick Brain Dump
Before we start:
- What's still in your head that needs capturing?
- Any loose threads you're worried about?
- Anything urgent you almost forgot?

*Get it out of your head and into the system*

### Step 2: Review Today's Wins
Look back at the day:
- What did you accomplish today?
- What moved forward, even slightly?
- Any unexpected wins?

*Find at least one win, even on hard days*

### Step 3: Check Against Intentions
Compare to morning startup:
- Did you complete your ONE Thing?
- Must-do's status?
- What got done vs. what didn't?

*No judgment, just awareness*

### Step 4: Capture Tomorrow's Priority
Before you forget:
- What's the single most important thing for tomorrow?
- Is there anything you need to prep tonight?
- Any early meetings that need attention?

### Step 5: Quick Calendar Check
Look at tomorrow:
- What meetings are on the calendar?
- Any conflicts or concerns?
- Does the day look manageable?

### Step 6: Clear the Workspace
Digital and physical:
- Close unnecessary tabs
- Save/commit any work in progress
- Clear physical desk if needed
- Set up for tomorrow's focus

### Step 7: Gratitude + Transition
Close out mentally:
- One thing you're grateful for from today
- Set an intention for the evening (rest, family, hobby)
- Give yourself permission to be done

## Variations

### Quick Close (5 min)
Just steps 4, 5, 7 - tomorrow's priority, calendar check, gratitude

### Extended Close (20 min)
Add journaling about the day

### Friday Close
Add weekly review elements

---

## Notes

- Don't let this become another task - keep it light
- The point is mental transition, not documentation
- If something urgent surfaces, note it for tomorrow (don't do it now)
- The "permission to be done" step is important
