## Section 1: Executive Summary

IAML is the premier intensive employment law education provider for HR professionals, benefits specialists, and in-house counsel. Since 1979, we have trained more than 80,000 professionals through multi-day programs taught by practicing attorneys from the nation's top employment law firms.

This playbook governs how we find, engage, qualify, and convert prospects into program registrants, and how we turn registrants into long-term advocates who refer colleagues and return for additional programs.

### The Strategy in One Paragraph

We segment every contact into one of three tiers based on their title and buying behavior. Directors (Tier 1) are our primary focus because they attend programs themselves and send their teams. Executives (Tier 2) control training budgets and decide where their organizations invest in development. Managers (Tier 3) are our volume attendees but need approval from above. Each tier receives different messaging, different offers, and different escalation triggers. Every interaction leads with value, not a sales pitch.

### The Four Audiences

| Audience | Strategy | Primary Offer |
|---|---|---|
| **New Prospects (Tier 1-3)** | Cold multi-channel outreach via SmartLead + HeyReach, escalating to personal GHL outreach on engagement | Complimentary Quarterly Employment Law Update ($397 value) as entry point |
| **Alumni / Past Participants** | Re-engagement campaign via SmartLead (IAML-branded domains) + LinkedIn, leading with complimentary Quarterly Updates and alumni discounts | Complimentary Quarterly Updates + alumni discount on next program |
| **Local Targets** | Geographic campaigns timed 3+ months before local programs, with post-program nurture drip | State-specific compliance content + local program invitation |
| **Non-Converters** | Long-term nurture drip with educational content, schedule updates, and periodic re-engagement | Value content (case digests, compliance alerts, checklists) |

### The Platform Flow

Cold outreach happens on marketing domains through SmartLead (email) and HeyReach (LinkedIn). These platforms are automation. The moment a prospect engages, replies, or shows strong interest signals, they are handed off to GoHighLevel where all communication comes from iaml.com. This transition is where automation ends and conversation begins. Gemini AI classifies every reply and routes contacts into the correct GHL branch automatically.

### The Offer Ladder

Every tier follows a progression from free to paid:

1. **Free value content** (compliance alerts, checklists, case digests)
2. **Complimentary Quarterly Employment Law Update** (90 minutes, normally $397, demonstrates IAML quality)
3. **Complimentary virtual program block** (zero cost to IAML, $575-$1,375 value, high-value trial for engaged contacts)
4. **Paid block or 2-day program** (lower commitment entry to paid programs)
5. **Full certificate program** ($2,375, the core product)
6. **Team registrations and corporate training** (expansion play)

No contact receives a paid pitch before receiving value first. This is non-negotiable.

### Volume and Scaling

We currently operate 50 active SmartLead inboxes. This playbook is built to scale. Volume targets are defined in three phases:

- **Current state:** 50 inboxes, ~1,000-1,250 emails/day, conservative sending limits
- **90-day target:** Optimize deliverability, increase per-inbox volume to 30-40/day, expand to 60 inboxes
- **180-day target:** 70+ inboxes, 2,000+ emails/day, full geographic coverage for all program cities

### Success Metrics (Summary)

| Metric | Target | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Quarterly Update signups from outreach | 50+/month | Weekly |
| Conversion: Quarterly Update to paid program | 10%+ | Monthly |
| Alumni re-engagement rate | 25%+ open, 5%+ reply | Monthly |

### How to Use This Playbook

This is an operational document. Every section contains specific copy, specific timing, and specific decision triggers. If you are executing outreach, start with Section 3 (Outreach Sequences by Tier) for your target audience. If you are handling a reply, go to Section 4 (Pipeline & Stages) to determine next steps. ## Section 2: Target Tier Profiles

### TIER 1: DIRECTORS

**Titles:** HR Director, Director of Employee Relations, Director of HR Operations, Director of People, Director of Total Rewards, Director of Talent, Senior HR Manager

**Who they are.** Mid-career to senior HR professionals. They've been in HR for 10-20 years. They manage a team of 3-15 HR generalists, managers, and specialists. They report to a VP or CHRO. They are the operational backbone of their HR function. They handle the hard stuff: investigations, terminations, FMLA disputes, ADA accommodations, policy rewrites. They are constantly worried about getting something wrong.

**Their daily reality.**
- They are busy. Not executive-busy (meetings all day), but execution-busy. They are in the weeds on employee relations cases, policy questions, and compliance reviews.
- They get pulled into legal-adjacent decisions regularly and often feel underqualified for the legal complexity involved.
- They attend SHRM conferences but find the sessions too broad. Ninety minutes on a topic they need ninety hours on.
- They want to be seen as strategic by their VP/CHRO but feel stuck in reactive mode.
- They have some budget authority. They can usually approve $2,000-$3,000 for their own development without executive sign-off. For team training, they need VP approval but their recommendation carries significant weight.

**What they care about.**
- Not making a costly legal mistake (fear-driven)
- Building confidence in employment law so they can advise managers without calling outside counsel every time (competence-driven)
- Career advancement to VP level (ambition-driven)
- Earning SHRM/HRCI credits efficiently (credential-driven)
- Practical tools they can use immediately, not academic theory

**What they do NOT care about.**
- Thought leadership buzzwords ("future of work," "employee experience transformation")
- Networking for networking's sake
- Generic HR conferences with vendor booths
- Content that talks down to them or assumes they are beginners

**Pain points we solve.**
- "I spend too much time on the phone with outside counsel for questions I should be able to answer myself."
- "I handled an investigation last month and I'm still not sure I did it right."
- "FMLA and ADA overlap situations paralyze me."
- "I went to SHRM but didn't learn anything I can actually apply."
- "I need real credits, not webinar credits."

**Messaging framework.**

*Subject line themes:*
- Specificity over cleverness: "FMLA + ADA overlap: the 3 scenarios that trip up most HR directors"
- Peer framing: "What 80,000 HR professionals learned about investigation mistakes"
- Local relevance: "Atlanta employment law training, September 2026"
- Direct value: "Complimentary 90-minute employment law update for HR directors ($397 value)"

*Opening hooks:*
- Problem recognition: "If you've ever hesitated before answering an FMLA question from a manager, you're not alone."
- Social proof: "Most of the 80,000 HR professionals we've trained tell us the same thing: they didn't know what they didn't know until they sat in our program."
- Direct offer: "I'd like to invite you to our next Quarterly Employment Law Update, normally $397 per session, complimentary for you. 90 minutes, 1.5 SHRM/HRCI credits, taught by practicing attorneys."

*Value propositions (in order of resonance):*
1. Confidence to handle complex employment law situations without outside counsel
2. Practical, attorney-taught training (not academic, not theoretical)
3. Multi-day depth (not 90-minute overviews)
4. 29.75 SHRM/HRCI/CLE credits in 4.5 days
5. 45-year track record, 80,000+ professionals trained

**Offer progression with timing.**

| Stage | Offer | Timing | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Value content (compliance alert, checklist, case digest) | Day 1 of outreach | SmartLead or HeyReach |
| 2 | Complimentary Quarterly Employment Law Update ($397 value) | Day 3-5 | SmartLead or HeyReach |
| 3 | Follow-up on Quarterly Update offer | Day 8-10 | SmartLead or HeyReach |
| 4 | GHL handoff (on reply or strong engagement signal) | On trigger | GHL, from Mike personally |
| 5 | Complimentary virtual program block (if engaged but not converting to paid) | After Quarterly Update attendance or 2+ positive interactions | GHL, from Mike |
| 6 | Paid program recommendation (block or full certificate) | After free block or strong buying signals | GHL, from Mike |
| 7 | Team registration conversation | After personal attendance | GHL or phone, from Mike |

**When to escalate to personal outreach (GHL).**
- They reply to any SmartLead or HeyReach message (any reply, even "not now")
- They open 3+ emails and click at least once
- They accept a LinkedIn connection and view your profile
- They register for the Quarterly Update

**When to call.**
- They replied positively but haven't registered for anything after 7 days
- They attended the Quarterly Update (call within 48 hours to discuss next steps)
- They are at a Fortune 500 or target company
- They replied "not now" with genuine interest (call in 30 days)

**When to stop.**
- No opens after full SmartLead sequence (move to national drip)
- Replied "not interested" or "remove me" (remove immediately, comply with request)
- Bounced email, no LinkedIn profile found (archive)
- Completed full GHL sequence with no engagement (move to national drip, try again in 6 months)

### TIER 2: EXECUTIVES

**Titles:** VP of HR, SVP of HR, CHRO, Chief People Officer, EVP of Human Resources

**Who they are.** Senior leaders who own the HR function for their organization. They manage directors who manage teams. They set strategy, control budgets, and report to the CEO or COO. They are not in the weeds on individual employee relations cases anymore. They think in terms of organizational risk, talent strategy, and leadership development.

**Their daily reality.**
- Back-to-back meetings. Their calendar is their constraint.
- They approve training budgets but rarely choose specific programs. Their directors recommend, they approve.
- They are evaluated on organizational metrics: retention, engagement scores, legal exposure, diversity goals.
- They delegate most operational HR work but stay close to high-risk situations (executive terminations, discrimination claims, union activity).
- They get pitched constantly. Vendors, consultants, software companies. Their inbox is a war zone.

**What they care about.**
- Reducing organizational legal exposure (risk-driven)
- Developing their HR team's capabilities so the team can handle more without escalating (efficiency-driven)
- Having a trusted external resource for employment law education (partnership-driven)
- ROI on training spend (budget-driven)
- Their own credibility with the CEO and board (reputation-driven)

**What they do NOT care about.**
- Attending training themselves (they're past that stage in their career, in their view)
- Individual program details (blocks, schedules, specific topics)
- SHRM/HRCI credits (they already have their credentials or don't need them)
- Being sold to

**Pain points we solve.**
- "My HR directors are good at HR but weak on the legal side. They call outside counsel too often."
- "I need my team to handle investigations confidently so I'm not pulled into every one."
- "Training budgets are tight. I need to justify every dollar."
- "We had a compliance issue last year that could have been prevented with better training."
- "I want one provider I can trust for employment law education, not a different vendor every quarter."

**Messaging framework.**

*Subject line themes:*
- Team development: "Developing your HR team's employment law confidence"
- Risk reduction: "Reducing your organization's employment law exposure"
- ROI: "What IAML's training saves in outside counsel fees"
- Peer framing: "How CHROs at [industry] companies develop their HR teams"

*Opening hooks:*
- Team-focused: "The strongest HR teams we work with have one thing in common: their directors can handle complex employment law situations without calling outside counsel. That confidence comes from training."
- Risk-focused: "Most employment law mistakes happen not because HR doesn't care, but because they weren't trained on the nuance. IAML exists to close that gap."
- Partnership-focused: "I'm reaching out because we work with HR leaders who want a single trusted resource for their team's employment law education."

*Value propositions (in order of resonance):*
1. Develop your HR team's ability to handle legal complexity independently
2. Reduce reliance on (and spending on) outside counsel for routine employment law questions
3. 45-year track record, faculty from the nation's top employment law firms
4. Flexible formats: in-person, virtual, on-demand, on-site for your team
5. One training partner for your entire HR team's employment law needs

**Offer progression with timing.**

| Stage | Offer | Timing | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Value content framed around team development or organizational risk | Day 1 | SmartLead or HeyReach |
| 2 | Complimentary Quarterly Update invitation ($397 value, positioned as: "see the quality before committing your team") | Day 3-5 | SmartLead or HeyReach |
| 3 | Offer to send one team member to a complimentary virtual program block | After engagement (reply, click, Quarterly Update attendance) | GHL, from Mike |

**When to escalate to personal outreach (GHL).**
- Any reply at all
- They open 3+ emails (even without clicking). Executives don't click, they open and skim.
- They accept LinkedIn connection
- Their title is CHRO or CPO at a company with 500+ employees (escalate immediately, do not wait for engagement signals)

**When to call.**
- They reply positively (call within 24 hours, executives move fast and forget fast)
- They attend the Quarterly Update (call within 24 hours)
- Fortune 500 CHRO/CPO who accepts LinkedIn connection (call within 48 hours)
- Their direct report (a Tier 1 director) has already engaged with IAML (warm introduction context)

**When to stop.**
- No opens after full sequence (move to national drip)
- Replied "not interested" (remove immediately)
- Assistant/gatekeeper replied "not interested" (remove immediately)
- Completed full GHL sequence with no engagement (national drip, try again in 6 months with a different angle)

### TIER 3: MANAGERS

**Titles:** HR Manager, HR Generalist, HR Business Partner, Employee Relations Manager, Benefits Manager, Compensation Manager

**Who they are.** Early to mid-career HR professionals. They've been in HR for 3-10 years. They handle day-to-day HR operations: onboarding, basic employee relations, benefits administration, policy enforcement. They report to a director or VP. They are building their careers and want to be taken seriously as professionals.

**Their daily reality.**
- They are the ones doing the work. Every FMLA request, every ADA accommodation, every employee complaint lands on their desk first.
- They often feel undertrained for the legal questions they face. They Google employment law questions more than they'd admit.
- They want professional development but feel guilty asking for it because of budget and time constraints.
- They look up to their director and want to get promoted to that level.
- They are active on LinkedIn and consume HR content regularly.

**What they care about.**
- Career advancement (getting promoted to director)
- Filling knowledge gaps that make them feel insecure
- Earning SHRM-CP/SCP or PHR/SPHR credentials (or maintaining them with credits)
- Being seen as competent and reliable by their boss
- Affordable, practical training that doesn't require weeks away from the office

**What they do NOT care about.**
- C-suite strategy discussions
- Organizational risk framing (that's their boss's problem)
- Corporate training partnerships (they don't control those decisions)
- Premium pricing signals (they see $2,375 and think "my boss will never approve that")

**Pain points we solve.**
- "I don't know enough employment law to feel confident giving advice to managers."
- "I want to get my SHRM-SCP but I need credits and I need to actually learn, not just check a box."
- "I can't be away from the office for 4.5 days. My boss would never approve that."
- "I found a program I want to attend but I don't know how to pitch it to my director."
- "I've been Googling employment law questions and I know that's not good enough."

**Messaging framework.**

*Subject line themes:*
- Career advancement: "The employment law knowledge gap holding most HR managers back"
- Practical skills: "Stop Googling FMLA questions. Here's a better way."
- Credential-focused: "Earn 13 SHRM/HRCI credits in 2 days"
- Low barrier: "2-day employment law program, virtual option available"

*Opening hooks:*
- Empathy: "If you've ever Googled an employment law question and hoped you got the right answer, you're not alone. Most HR professionals tell us the same thing."
- Career framing: "The HR managers who get promoted to director all have one thing in common: they invested in building their employment law expertise early."
- Practical: "In 2 days, you can build more employment law confidence than most HR professionals build in 2 years of on-the-job learning."

*Value propositions (in order of resonance):*
1. Build employment law confidence that accelerates your career
2. 2-day programs that fit your schedule and your budget
3. Taught by practicing attorneys, not academics
4. SHRM/HRCI credits that count toward your certification
5. Virtual option so you don't have to travel

**A/B test: Full programs vs. blocks.**

This is a Tier 3 specific test. Build two versions of the outreach sequence:

- **Version A:** Lead with individual blocks or 2-day programs ($575-$1,575, 0.5-2.5 days, 6.5-13 credits). Position it as accessible, practical, fits your schedule.
- **Version B:** Lead with full certificate program ($2,375, 4.5 days, 29.75 credits). Position it as the gold standard.

**Hypothesis:** Version A will generate higher reply rates and more registrations from Tier 3 because the financial and time commitment is lower and easier to get approved.

**How to test:** Split Tier 3 contacts 50/50. Run both versions for 60 days. Measure reply rate, Quarterly Update signups, and paid registrations. The winner becomes the default Tier 3 approach.

**Offer progression with timing.**

| Stage | Offer | Timing | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Educational content (employment law tip, checklist, case study) | Day 1 | SmartLead or HeyReach |
| 2 | Complimentary Quarterly Employment Law Update ($397 value) | Day 3-5 | SmartLead or HeyReach |
| 3 | Program recommendation (block or full, per A/B test) | Day 8-12 | SmartLead |
| 4 | GHL handoff on engagement | On trigger | GHL, from IAML (not Mike personally) |
| 5 | Complimentary virtual block (only if Quarterly Update attended and highly engaged) | After Quarterly Update | GHL, from IAML |
| 6 | Paid block or 2-day program | After engagement | GHL |
| 7 | "Convince My Boss" tool (template they can use to build a business case for their director) | When they express interest but say they need approval | GHL |

**Stage 7 is critical for Tier 3.** These contacts want to attend but can't approve the spend. The Convince My Boss tool generates a professional business case document they can send to their director that explains the ROI, the credits, and the time commitment. That document also introduces IAML to a Tier 1 or Tier 2 decision-maker organically.

**When to escalate to personal outreach (GHL).**
- They reply to any message
- They register for the Quarterly Update
- They click on a program link twice or more

**When to call.**
- Almost never for Tier 3. They don't expect it and it can feel aggressive.
- Exception: They replied saying "I want to attend but need approval." In that case, offer to call their director directly to explain the program. (This also opens a Tier 1/2 relationship.)
- Exception: They used the Convince My Boss tool (call within 3 days, they are very high intent).

**When to stop.**
- No opens after full sequence (move to national drip)
- Replied "not interested" (remove)
- Completed full GHL sequence with no engagement (national drip)

### TIER 4: ALUMNI / PAST PARTICIPANTS

**Who they are.** 3,500-5,000 people who have attended an IAML program at some point. They span all title tiers. They already know the brand, the quality, and the faculty. They are your warmest audience and your best source of referrals, repeat registrations, and corporate account expansion.

**Their relationship to IAML.**
- They attended a program and (in most cases) had a positive experience
- They may have lost touch. Some attended years ago.
- They may have changed jobs, gotten promoted, or moved to a new company
- They are the most credible advocates for IAML when talking to peers

**What they care about.**
- Staying current (employment law changes constantly)
- Maintaining SHRM/HRCI credits
- Advancing their careers (many have been promoted since attending)
- Getting value from a brand they already trust

**Re-engagement campaign structure.**

| Phase | Action | Timing | Channel |

| 2 | Follow-up with a specific value asset (compliance alert, case digest) | Week 2 | SmartLead |

## Section 4: Pipeline & Stage Definitions

Every contact exists in exactly one stage at any time. Stages are sequential but contacts can skip forward (a cold lead who replies "I want to register" jumps straight to Opportunity) or loop back (a stalled Opportunity who goes quiet re-enters Nurture).

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                     │
│  UNCONTACTED --> PROSPECTING --> ENGAGED --> OPPORTUNITY --> WON     │
│       |              |              |             |                  │
│       |              |              |             |                  │
│       v              v              v             v                  │
│   (archived)    (national      (national     (stalled/              │
│                  drip)          drip)         lost)                  │
│                                                                     │
│  ALUMNI RE-ENGAGEMENT flows into ENGAGED or OPPORTUNITY directly    │
│  COLLEAGUE EXPANSION flows into PROSPECTING or OPPORTUNITY          │
│                                                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### Stage 1: UNCONTACTED

**Definition:** Contact exists in your database but has never received outreach from any channel.

**How they enter:**
- Imported from Sales Navigator search
- Imported from SmartLead lead database
- Enriched via Hunter.io
- Added from alumni list (before re-engagement campaign launches)
- Added from colleague expansion trigger (before first email sends)

**Data required before moving forward:**
- Email address (verified, not bounced)
- Full name
- Title (mapped to tier)
- Company name
- Location (state, for geographic campaign targeting)

**What happens here:**
- Contact is assigned a tier (1, 2, or 3) based on title
- Contact is assigned a campaign type based on context:
  - Alumni re-engagement (if past participant)
  - Colleague expansion (if triggered by a colleague's registration)
  - Local program campaign (if in a target geography with an upcoming program 3+ months out)
  - Core cold outreach (if none of the above apply)
- Contact is deduplicated against existing database (no double-contacting)

**Exit criteria:**
- First email sent OR first LinkedIn connection request sent -> moves to PROSPECTING
- Email bounces on first send -> moves to ARCHIVED
- Duplicate found -> merged or deleted

**Maximum time in this stage:** Contacts should not sit here longer than 7 days.

---

### Stage 2: PROSPECTING

**Definition:** Contact is in an active outreach sequence (SmartLead, HeyReach, or both) but has not yet shown any engagement signal.

**Engagement signals we're watching for:**

| Signal | Strength | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Opens 1 email | Weak | No action, continue sequence |
| Opens 3+ emails | Moderate | Flag for monitoring. If Tier 1 or 2, consider adding LinkedIn. |
| Clicks any link | Strong | Move to ENGAGED |
| Replies (any reply) | Strong | Move to ENGAGED, trigger GHL handoff and AI classification |
| Accepts LinkedIn connection | Moderate | Continue HeyReach sequence |
| Replies on LinkedIn | Strong | Move to ENGAGED, trigger GHL handoff |
| Registers for Quarterly Update | Very strong | Move to ENGAGED immediately |
| Bounced email | Terminal | Move to ARCHIVED |
| Unsubscribe/opt-out | Terminal | Remove immediately, move to DO NOT CONTACT |

**Exit criteria:**
- Any strong engagement signal -> ENGAGED
- Sequence completes with no engagement -> NATIONAL DRIP (after 7-day cool-down)
- Bounced/unsubscribe -> ARCHIVED or DO NOT CONTACT

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|---|---|---|

## Section 5: Offer Strategy

### The Offer Philosophy

Every interaction leads with value. This is a deliberate strategy based on three principles:

1. **Reciprocity.** When you give someone a $397 Quarterly Update for free, they feel a natural obligation to engage further.
2. **Risk removal.** Every free offer exists to answer "Will this actually be good?" before they spend a dollar.
3. **Ascending commitment.** People don't go from stranger to $2,375 in one step. Each step is small and builds on the previous one.

### The Complete Offer Ladder

| Offer | Value | Cost to IAML | Who Gets It | When |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Free value content (checklists, alerts, digests) | Moderate | Zero | Everyone, all tiers | Entry point, first email |
| Complimentary Quarterly Update (single session) | $397 | Zero | All prospects, all tiers | First or second email |
| Complimentary Quarterly Updates (12 months, 4 sessions) | $1,588 | Zero | Anyone who enrolls in a paid program | Included with paid enrollment |
| Complimentary virtual program block | $575-$1,375 | Zero | Engaged Tier 1 directors, one team member of engaged Tier 2 executives | After QU attendance or 2+ positive interactions |
| Alumni/colleague discount: 4.5-day certificate | $500 off ($2,375 -> $1,875) | $500 | Past participants and same-company colleagues | On re-engagement or colleague trigger |
| Alumni/colleague discount: 2-day program | $300 off ($1,575 -> $1,275) | $300 | Past participants and same-company colleagues | On re-engagement or colleague trigger |
| Alumni/colleague discount: individual block | $100 off | $100 | Past participants and same-company colleagues | On re-engagement or colleague trigger |
| Convince My Boss tool | Zero cost | Zero | Tier 3 managers needing approval | When they express interest but need boss sign-off |

**The $397 anchor must appear every time.** If you don't mention the price, it feels like a free marketing event. When you mention the price, it feels like a professional courtesy.

## Section 7: Objection Handling Guide

For every objection: **Acknowledge** (show you heard them) -> **Clarify** (understand the real issue) -> **Reframe** (present from a different angle) -> **Offer a path forward** (specific, low-pressure next step).

Not a rejection. This is a buying signal with a process blocker. Send Convince My Boss tool. Offer to call the decision-maker. Follow up at Day 5, 10, 15, and 21.

Clarify: genuine timing or polite decline? If they give a specific timeframe, PARK them with a follow-up date and offer the Quarterly Update in the meantime. If vague, move to National Drip.

### Objection 8: "Just send me information."

### Objection 9: "I've never heard of IAML."

"Absolutely. I'll tag you for programs in [state/city]. In the meantime, the Quarterly Update is virtual and complimentary ($397 value)." Move to National Drip with geographic tag.
