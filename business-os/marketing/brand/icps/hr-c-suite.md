# ICP: HR C-Suite

> Chief Human Resources Officers and VP-level HR leaders with strategic and budget authority.

---

## Profile Overview

**Segment Name:** HR C-Suite
**Role/Title:** CHRO, Chief People Officer, VP of Human Resources, SVP HR
**Industry Focus:** All industries
**Organization Size:** Mid-Market to Enterprise (500+ employees)

---

## Demographics

### Professional Profile
- **Typical Titles:** CHRO, Chief People Officer, Chief Human Resources Officer, VP Human Resources, SVP People & Culture
- **Years of Experience:** 15-30+ years
- **Reports To:** CEO, COO, or Board
- **Team Size:** 5-100+ (full HR organization)
- **Budget Authority:** Yes - full HR budget authority

### Organization Context
- **Company Size:** 500-100,000+ employees
- **Industry:** All industries
- **Geography:** Often multi-location, potentially global

---

## Psychographics

### Goals & Motivations
1. **Strategic workforce enablement** - HR as business driver, not just admin function
2. **Organizational culture** - Build and protect company culture
3. **Risk mitigation** - Protect organization from people-related liability
4. **Talent advantage** - Attract, develop, retain best people
5. **Executive credibility** - Be seen as strategic partner at leadership table

### Challenges & Pain Points
1. **Doing more with less** - Budget pressure with expanding mandate
2. **Evolving workforce expectations** - Remote work, DEI, wellbeing
3. **Compliance complexity** - Multi-state/country regulatory requirements
4. **Manager capability gaps** - Frontline leaders who can't manage people effectively
5. **Demonstrating ROI** - Proving HR's value to the business

### Fears & Concerns
1. **Major compliance failure** - EEOC investigation, class action
2. **Culture erosion** - Losing what makes the company special
3. **Key talent loss** - Losing critical employees to competitors
4. **Being seen as cost center** - Not valued strategically
5. **Career stagnation** - Being typecast as "just HR"

---

## Buying Behavior

### Decision-Making Style
- **Process:** Strategic, outcome-focused, delegates details
- **Timeline:** 2-8 weeks (longer for enterprise-wide initiatives)
- **Influencers:** Legal, Finance, CEO, HR team
- **Approval Needed From:** May need CEO/CFO for large initiatives

### Triggers for Action
- New company strategy requiring workforce capabilities
- Merger/acquisition creating integration needs
- Compliance issue or near-miss
- High-profile competitor doing something they're not
- Board pressure on HR metrics
- New legislation affecting workforce

### Objections & Concerns
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We have internal L&D" | "We augment your team with specialized expertise they don't need to maintain in-house" |
| "Need to see ROI" | "Let's design measurement into the program - here's how other CHROs have tracked impact" |
| "Too busy for major initiative" | "We handle everything - your team's involvement is minimal" |
| "Already working with a vendor" | "Happy to complement - we specialize in [specific area] that may be a gap" |

## Communication Preferences

### Channels
- **Primary:** Email (direct, executive assistant may screen)
- **Secondary:** LinkedIn (executive network), Referral (peer introductions)
- **Avoid:** Cold calls (heavily screened), mass marketing (seen as junior)

### Content Preferences
- **Format:** Executive summaries, peer roundtables, strategic thought leadership
- **Length:** Short - executive summary format, option to go deeper
- **Tone:** Strategic, business-focused, peer-to-peer, outcome-oriented

### Best Times to Reach
- **Day of Week:** Tuesday-Thursday
- **Time of Day:** Early morning or after business hours (when they read email)
- **Avoid:** Budget season, annual review periods

## Competitive Considerations

### Alternatives They Consider
- Big consulting firms (McKinsey, Deloitte) - expensive, overkill for training
- HR tech platforms with training (Workday, SAP) - generic content
- Industry-specific training firms - may lack legal expertise
- University executive education - expensive, time-intensive

### Why They Choose Us
- Specialized expertise (employment law + HR + training)
- Scalable (can train entire organization)
- Customizable to their culture and policies
- Results-focused with measurement

### Why They Might Not Choose Us
- Existing vendor relationship
- Building internal capability
- Budget constraints
- Not prioritizing training currently

## Example Persona

**Name:** Marcus Williams
**Title:** Chief People Officer
**Company:** Multi-state retail company (8,000 employees, 200 locations)

**Bio:**
Marcus joined as CPO 3 years ago to "modernize HR." He's focused on making HR a strategic function but spends too much time on compliance issues and manager escalations. He has a strong L&D team but they lack employment law expertise. He needs to show the board progress on reducing turnover and employment claims.

**Quote:**
"I didn't take this job to spend my time reviewing termination paperwork. I need managers who can handle their own people issues correctly the first time."

## Notes & Observations

- Peer referrals are gold - CHROs trust other CHROs
- Often have a trusted HR advisor/consultant - getting their endorsement helps
- Think enterprise-wide, not individual programs
- Want to be seen as innovative, forward-thinking
- Respond well to "what are other CHROs doing" positioning
