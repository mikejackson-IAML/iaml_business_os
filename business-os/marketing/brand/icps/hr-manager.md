# ICP: HR Manager

> Frontline HR leaders handling day-to-day operations and employee relations.

---

## Profile Overview

**Segment Name:** HR Manager
**Role/Title:** HR Manager, HR Business Partner, Employee Relations Manager
**Industry Focus:** All industries
**Organization Size:** All sizes (100+ employees)

---

## Demographics

### Professional Profile
- **Typical Titles:** HR Manager, HR Business Partner, Employee Relations Manager, HR Generalist (Senior)
- **Years of Experience:** 5-15 years
- **Reports To:** HR Director, VP HR, or CHRO
- **Team Size:** 0-5 (may have coordinators/generalists)
- **Budget Authority:** Limited - may influence but not approve

### Organization Context
- **Company Size:** 100-5,000 employees (or business unit)
- **Industry:** All industries
- **Geography:** Single location or regional

---

## Psychographics

### Goals & Motivations
1. **Handle employee issues effectively** - Resolve problems without escalation
2. **Be seen as trusted advisor** - Go-to person for managers
3. **Career growth** - Path to Director
4. **Stay current** - Keep up with HR developments
5. **Work-life balance** - Not drowning in crises

### Challenges & Pain Points
1. **Constant interruptions** - Managers with questions all day
2. **Complex situations** - Cases that don't fit neat categories
3. **Manager capability gaps** - Doing their work for them
4. **Documentation burden** - Paperwork overload
5. **Limited authority** - Need to escalate too often

### Fears & Concerns
1. **Making a mistake on a complex case**
2. **Missing something that becomes a lawsuit**
3. **Burned out from constant firefighting**
4. **Career stuck at manager level**
5. **Not knowing the answer when asked**

---

## Buying Behavior

### Decision-Making Style
- **Process:** Practical, needs-based, often responding to specific problem
- **Timeline:** 1-3 weeks (or immediate if crisis)
- **Influencers:** HR Director, peers, legal
- **Approval Needed From:** HR Director or VP for any spending

### Triggers for Action
- Specific incident they couldn't handle well
- Pattern of same issues recurring
- Compliance requirement they're unsure about
- New responsibility given without training
- Performance review identified development need

### Objections & Concerns
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I need to ask my director" | "Of course - want me to prepare a summary for them?" |
| "No budget" | "Would your director support if we showed the business case?" |
| "I'm too busy" | "Virtual options fit around your schedule - even 30 min sessions" |
| "I should know this already" | "Even experienced HR pros need to stay current - laws change constantly" |

---

## Communication Preferences

### Channels
- **Primary:** Email
- **Secondary:** LinkedIn, Webinars
- **Tertiary:** HR community groups (SHRM local chapters, online communities)

### Content Preferences
- **Format:** Quick tips, checklists, how-to guides, short webinars
- **Length:** Short to medium - practical and actionable
- **Tone:** Supportive, educational, peer-level, "we've all been there"

### Best Times to Reach
- **Day of Week:** Tuesday-Thursday
- **Time of Day:** Early morning or lunch hour (when not in meetings)
- **Avoid:** Monday AM and Friday PM

---

## Messaging Framework

### Value Proposition
"Get the knowledge and confidence to handle complex HR situations without constantly escalating or worrying you'll make a costly mistake."

### Key Messages
1. **Confidence:** "Know you're handling situations correctly, even the complex ones."
2. **Efficiency:** "Stop spending hours researching every question - have answers ready."
3. **Career development:** "Build the expertise that leads to Director-level roles."

### Proof Points
- "HR Managers report 60% faster resolution of employee issues after training"
- "Build your expertise in [hot topic] that directors are looking for"
- [Peer testimonial about career impact]

### Call-to-Action
"What's the trickiest HR situation you've dealt with recently? Let's talk about how to handle those better."

## Example Persona

**Name:** David Thompson
**Title:** HR Manager
**Company:** Healthcare services company (600 employees)

**Bio:**
David has been an HR Manager for 4 years, working his way up from Generalist. He handles employee relations for 3 departments and supports about 50 managers. He's good at his job but constantly feels like he's one complex case away from being in over his head. He wants to get to Director but knows he needs to level up his employment law knowledge.

**Quote:**
"I can handle 90% of what comes across my desk, but that 10% keeps me up at night. What if I get it wrong?"

## Notes & Observations

- Usually not the budget holder but strong influencer
- Often champions training requests to director
- Very active in HR communities (SHRM, LinkedIn groups)
- Appreciate practical, immediately applicable content
- Certification (SHRM-CP, PHR) often important to them
- May attend open enrollment programs individually
