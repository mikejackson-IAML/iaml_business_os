# ICP: HR Senior

> Senior HR professionals below director level with significant experience and influence.

---

## Profile Overview

**Segment Name:** HR Senior
**Role/Title:** Senior HR Manager, Senior HR Business Partner, Senior HR Generalist
**Industry Focus:** All industries
**Organization Size:** Mid-Market to Enterprise (500+ employees)

---

## Demographics

### Professional Profile
- **Typical Titles:** Senior HR Manager, Senior HRBP, Senior HR Generalist, HR Lead, HR Program Manager
- **Years of Experience:** 8-15 years
- **Reports To:** HR Director, VP HR
- **Team Size:** 0-5 (may lead junior HR staff)
- **Budget Authority:** Some - project budgets, training recommendations

### Organization Context
- **Company Size:** 500-10,000 employees (or business unit)
- **Industry:** All industries
- **Geography:** Single to multi-location

---

## Psychographics

### Goals & Motivations
1. **Recognition as expert** - Go-to person for complex issues
2. **Path to Director** - Career advancement
3. **Reduce escalations** - Handle everything at their level
4. **Develop others** - Mentor junior HR staff
5. **Work-life balance** - Sustainable workload

### Challenges & Pain Points
1. **Complex cases without clear answers** - Gray areas in employment law
2. **Being stretched across too many areas** - Expected to know everything
3. **Career plateau risk** - Director roles are limited
4. **Manager skill gaps** - Doing their job for them
5. **Staying current** - Laws change constantly

### Fears & Concerns
1. **Making a mistake on a high-stakes case**
2. **Career stalling at senior level**
3. **Being seen as not strategic enough for Director**
4. **Burnout from complexity and volume**
5. **Knowledge becoming outdated**

---

## Buying Behavior

### Decision-Making Style
- **Process:** Expert-focused, wants depth not basics
- **Timeline:** 2-4 weeks
- **Influencers:** HR Director, Legal, peers
- **Approval Needed From:** HR Director for spending

### Triggers for Action
- Complex case they struggled with
- Feedback that they need deeper expertise
- Preparing for Director-level interview
- Significant law change in their specialty area
- New certification or development goal

### Objections & Concerns
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too basic for me" | "Our advanced programs are designed for experienced HR pros like you" |
| "Need director approval" | "Happy to help make the case - here's what peers have seen" |
| "I've been doing this for years" | "Exactly why advanced topics matter - basics aren't your gap" |
| "Budget constraints" | "Professional development investment - ROI in avoided mistakes" |

---

## Communication Preferences

### Channels
- **Primary:** Email, LinkedIn
- **Secondary:** HR communities, webinars
- **Tertiary:** Industry events

### Content Preferences
- **Format:** Deep-dive articles, case studies, advanced webinars
- **Length:** Medium to long - they want depth
- **Tone:** Expert-to-expert, nuanced, acknowledges their experience

### Best Times to Reach
- **Day of Week:** Tuesday-Thursday
- **Time of Day:** Mid-morning, early afternoon
- **Avoid:** Quarter-end periods

---

## Messaging Framework

### Value Proposition
"Deepen your employment law expertise to handle the complex cases that others escalate, positioning yourself as the go-to expert and ready for Director-level roles."

### Key Messages
1. **Expert status:** "Be the person everyone comes to for the tough cases."
2. **Career advancement:** "Build the expertise that Directors need."
3. **Complex case mastery:** "Handle gray areas with confidence."

### Proof Points
- "Senior HR pros report handling 40% more cases independently after advanced training"
- "Direct path to Director for those who master complex employment law"
- [Peer testimonial about career advancement]

### Call-to-Action
"What's the most complex HR situation you're dealing with right now? Let's talk about how to approach it."

## Example Persona

**Name:** Michael Chen
**Title:** Senior HR Business Partner
**Company:** Technology company (2,500 employees)

**Bio:**
Michael has 12 years in HR and has been a Senior HRBP for 3 years. He partners with 4 business units and handles the most complex cases that junior HRBPs escalate. He's known as the "employment law expert" on the team, but he knows he's not truly an expert - just more experienced than others. He's applying for Director roles and wants to be sure he can handle the increased complexity.

**Quote:**
"I'm good at this job, but Director-level issues are a whole different level of complexity. I want to be ready."

---

## Notes & Observations

- Wants to be treated as experienced professional, not beginner
- Certification (SHRM-SCP, SPHR) often already achieved or in progress
- Active in professional development - often self-motivated
- May attend open enrollment programs or advocate for team training
- Career development messaging resonates strongly
- Values recognition and expertise positioning
