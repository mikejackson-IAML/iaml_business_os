# ICP: HR Director

> Mid-senior HR leaders managing departments and driving HR initiatives with budget influence.

---

## Profile Overview

**Segment Name:** HR Director
**Role/Title:** HR Director, Director of People Operations, Director of Talent
**Industry Focus:** All industries
**Organization Size:** Mid-Market (200-2000 employees) or division of larger company

---

## Demographics

### Professional Profile
- **Typical Titles:** HR Director, Director of Human Resources, Director of People Operations, Regional HR Director
- **Years of Experience:** 10-20 years
- **Reports To:** VP HR, CHRO, or CEO (smaller companies)
- **Team Size:** 2-15 HR professionals
- **Budget Authority:** Yes - departmental budget, may need approval for larger initiatives

### Organization Context
- **Company Size:** 200-2,000 employees (or business unit of larger org)
- **Industry:** All industries
- **Geography:** Single or multi-location regional

---

## Psychographics

### Goals & Motivations
1. **Build a high-functioning HR team** - Develop their people
2. **Reduce risk and firefighting** - Proactive vs. reactive
3. **Career advancement** - Path to VP or CHRO
4. **Earn respect of business leaders** - Be seen as partner
5. **Create efficient processes** - Systematize HR

### Challenges & Pain Points
1. **Stretched too thin** - Too many priorities, not enough resources
2. **Manager problems landing on their desk** - Constant escalations
3. **Keeping up with compliance** - Regulatory changes
4. **Balancing strategic and tactical** - Stuck in weeds
5. **Proving value to leadership** - Fighting for budget

### Fears & Concerns
1. **Major compliance failure on their watch**
2. **Not developing fast enough for promotion**
3. **Team burnout or turnover**
4. **Being blamed for manager failures**
5. **Budget cuts to their department**

---

## Buying Behavior

### Decision-Making Style
- **Process:** Practical, ROI-focused, needs to justify to leadership
- **Timeline:** 2-4 weeks (faster if solving urgent problem)
- **Influencers:** Their team, Legal, Finance, peer HR directors
- **Approval Needed From:** VP/CHRO for larger investments

### Triggers for Action
- Compliance deadline approaching
- Incident that exposed training gap
- New state/location requirements
- Audit or review identified gaps
- Pressure from legal or risk management
- New role/promotion creating capability needs

### Objections & Concerns
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Need VP/CHRO approval" | "Happy to help with the business case - here's what's worked for others" |
| "Budget's already allocated" | "Could this fit under compliance/risk budget? Or a Q[X] initiative?" |
| "My team could do this" | "Free your team for strategic work - we handle the specialized training" |
| "We've tried training before" | "What didn't work? Let's design something different this time" |

---

## Communication Preferences

### Channels
- **Primary:** Email
- **Secondary:** LinkedIn, Webinars
- **Tertiary:** Industry conferences/events

### Content Preferences
- **Format:** Practical how-tos, templates, benchmarking, webinars
- **Length:** Medium - detailed enough to be useful
- **Tone:** Professional, practical, peer-level, solution-oriented

### Best Times to Reach
- **Day of Week:** Tuesday-Thursday
- **Time of Day:** Mid-morning or mid-afternoon
- **Avoid:** Open enrollment periods, annual review seasons

---

## Messaging Framework

### Value Proposition
"Get managers handling people issues correctly so your team can stop firefighting and focus on strategic initiatives that advance your career."

### Key Messages
1. **Efficiency:** "Every manager trained correctly is one less escalation on your desk."
2. **Compliance confidence:** "Know that your training meets legal requirements across all your locations."
3. **Team development:** "Programs that develop your team's expertise while training the organization."

### Proof Points
- "HR Directors report 40% reduction in manager escalations after training"
- "Our compliance tracking gives you audit-ready documentation"
- [Testimonial from peer HR Director]

### Call-to-Action
"Let's map your compliance calendar and identify the biggest gaps that training could solve."

## Program Fit

### Most Relevant Programs
| Program | Relevance | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Manager Training | 🔴 High | Biggest pain reliever |
| Harassment Prevention | 🔴 High | Required, they're responsible |
| HR Professional Development | 🔴 High | Team development |
| Employment Law Fundamentals | 🟠 Medium | For their team |
| FMLA/ADA/Leave | 🟠 Medium | Common escalation area |

### Delivery Preference
- **Format:** Virtual (easy logistics) or blended
- **Duration:** Half-day to full-day
- **Team vs. Individual:** Mix - team training for consistency, open enrollment for individuals

## Example Persona

**Name:** Jennifer Martinez
**Title:** HR Director
**Company:** Regional manufacturing company (800 employees, 4 locations)

**Bio:**
Jennifer has been HR Director for 5 years, promoted from HR Manager. She manages a team of 4 and is responsible for all HR functions across 4 plants in 3 states. She's stretched thin and frustrated that she spends more time on compliance and manager problems than on strategic initiatives. She wants to prove she's ready for VP.

**Quote:**
"I have so many ideas to make this company better, but I can't get to any of them because I'm constantly dealing with managers who don't know basic employment law."

---

## Notes & Observations

- Often the "champion buyer" who drives the decision even if CHRO approves
- Very responsive to peer recommendations and case studies
- Practical - wants to see exactly what they're getting
- Appreciate vendors who make them look good to leadership
- Multi-state/location = higher pain and value
