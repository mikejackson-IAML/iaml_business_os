# Brand Voice Guidelines

> How [COMPANY NAME] communicates as an organization across all channels.

---

## Voice Overview

### Who We Are
We are **trusted experts** in employment law, benefits law, HR management, and EHS training. We help organizations protect themselves and their employees through practical, engaging education.

### Our Voice Personality
We are:
- **Authoritative but Approachable** - Experts who don't talk down
- **Practical, Not Academic** - Real-world focused
- **Clear, Not Complex** - Make complicated simple
- **Confident, Not Arrogant** - Know our stuff without boasting
- **Supportive, Not Judgmental** - We've all been there

### We Are NOT
- Stiff, formal, or legalistic
- Salesy or pushy
- Generic or bland
- Preachy or condescending
- Overly casual or unprofessional

## Voice Attributes

### 1. EXPERT
We know our subjects deeply. We've seen it all. We stay current.

**Do:**
- Reference specific laws, cases, and regulations
- Share real-world examples and scenarios
- Cite specific data and research
- Use precise terminology correctly

**Don't:**
- Overuse jargon for its own sake
- Be vague when precision matters
- Claim expertise outside our domains
- Speak in generalities

**Example:**
```
✅ "PWFA took effect June 27, 2023, expanding accommodation requirements
   for pregnancy-related conditions. Here's what that means for your policies."

❌ "There's a new pregnancy law. You should probably update things."
```

### 2. PRACTICAL
We focus on what works in the real world, not just theory.

**Do:**
- Give actionable advice
- Provide templates, checklists, examples
- Address common scenarios
- Acknowledge real-world constraints

**Don't:**
- Be purely theoretical
- Ignore practical implementation
- Oversimplify complex situations
- Assume unlimited resources

**Example:**
```
✅ "Here's a 3-step process for handling an accommodation request
   that works whether you have 5 employees or 5,000."

❌ "Per EEOC guidelines, organizations should implement comprehensive
   interactive process procedures in accordance with established protocols."
```

### 3. CLEAR
We make complex topics understandable without dumbing them down.

**Do:**
- Use plain language first, then technical terms if needed
- Break down complex concepts
- Use analogies and examples
- Structure content logically

**Don't:**
- Assume knowledge without explanation
- Use jargon as a shortcut
- Bury key points
- Ramble or repeat

**Example:**
```
✅ "FMLA gives eligible employees up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected
   leave. Think of it as a pause button - their job is held while they
   take care of what they need to."

❌ "Under the FMLA's provisions for eligible employees as defined in
   29 CFR 825.110, designated leave entitlements of up to 12 workweeks..."
```

### 4. CONFIDENT
We know what we're talking about and aren't afraid to take positions.

**Do:**
- Make clear recommendations
- Take positions on best practices
- Share our perspective
- Speak directly

**Don't:**
- Hedge excessively
- Overuse qualifiers
- Avoid taking positions
- Sound uncertain

**Example:**
```
✅ "You should document every accommodation conversation. Period.
   It protects you and the employee."

❌ "It might be a good idea to maybe consider documenting accommodation
   conversations when possible."
```

### 5. SUPPORTIVE
We're partners, not critics. We help, not lecture.

**Do:**
- Acknowledge challenges
- Offer solutions, not just problems
- Celebrate wins
- Empathize with struggles

**Don't:**
- Shame for not knowing
- Focus only on what's wrong
- Be preachy
- Ignore emotional component

**Example:**
```
✅ "Complex leave situations are genuinely hard. Let's walk through
   how to approach them step by step."

❌ "Too many HR professionals don't understand basic leave law.
   Here's what you're probably doing wrong."
```

## Tone Variations by Context

Our core voice stays consistent, but tone adjusts:

| Context | Tone Shift | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Training/Education | More instructive, detailed | "Let's examine how this works..." |
| Marketing | More engaging, benefit-focused | "Protect your organization and your peace of mind" |
| Email Outreach | More conversational, direct | "Quick question about your compliance training..." |
| Social Media | More casual, topical | "New FMLA update just dropped. Here's what it means." |
| Proposals | More formal, client-focused | "We're pleased to present..." |
| Crisis/Compliance | More serious, precise | "Effective immediately, employers must..." |

## Language Guidelines

### Words We Use
- Protect (vs. avoid)
- Practical (vs. theoretical)
- Confident (vs. sure)
- Navigate (vs. deal with)
- Equip (vs. train)
- Partner (vs. vendor)

### Words We Avoid
- "Simple" (often isn't)
- "Just" (minimizes complexity)
- "Obviously" (condescending)
- "Comprehensive" (overused, means nothing)
- "Solutions" (generic)
- "Leverage" (corporate speak)

### Formatting Preferences
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Bullet points for lists
- Bold for emphasis, not CAPS
- Headers to organize content
- Numbers when sequence matters

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Email Outreach (Cold)
- Subject: Clear, specific, not clickbaity
- Opening: Direct, respect their time
- Body: Quick value, easy scan
- CTA: One clear action
- Length: Short (under 150 words)

### Email Nurture (Warm)
- More educational content
- Longer is acceptable if valuable
- Reference previous interactions
- Build relationship over time

### LinkedIn
- Hook in first line (visible before "see more")
- Personal and professional
- Engage, don't just broadcast
- Thought leadership, not selling

### Website/Blog
- SEO-friendly but human first
- Scannable structure
- Clear takeaways
- CTAs that match content

### Proposals
- Client's name and needs first
- Our qualifications second
- Clear pricing and options
- Professional formatting

## Example Transformations

### Before (Generic)
> "Our comprehensive training solutions help organizations with their compliance needs through our proven methodology."

### After (Our Voice)
> "We train your managers to handle employment law issues correctly - so they stop making the mistakes that create lawsuits."

---

### Before (Too Academic)
> "Research indicates that organizations implementing systematic compliance training programs experience a statistically significant reduction in employment-related litigation."

### After (Our Voice)
> "Companies that train their people on employment law see fewer lawsuits. It's that straightforward."

---

### Before (Too Casual)
> "Hey! Wanna avoid getting sued? We can totally help with that!"

### After (Our Voice)
> "Employment lawsuits are preventable. Let's talk about how proper training protects your organization."

## Review Checklist

Before publishing any content, ask:

- [ ] Does it sound like us (expert, practical, clear, confident, supportive)?
- [ ] Would our audience find this valuable?
- [ ] Is it clear what we want them to do or learn?
- [ ] Is it appropriately detailed without being overwhelming?
- [ ] Does it avoid our "don't" behaviors?
- [ ] Is it the right tone for this channel?
