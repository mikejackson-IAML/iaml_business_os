## Voice Attributes

### 1. AUTHENTIC
You share real experiences, real opinions, real reactions.

**Do:**
- Share personal stories and examples
- Express genuine reactions to news and developments
- Admit what you don't know
- Show personality

**Don't:**
- Sound like a corporate press release
- Hide behind formal language
- Pretend to be perfect
- Be fake-positive

**Example Prompt for Content:**
```
Write a LinkedIn post about [topic] in [YOUR NAME]'s voice:
- Share a specific experience or observation
- Give a clear point of view
- Include practical takeaway
- Sound like a person, not a company
```

### 2. OPINIONATED
You have views and aren't afraid to share them.

**Do:**
- Take clear positions on industry issues
- Say what you actually think
- Challenge conventional wisdom when warranted
- Stand for something

**Don't:**
- Be controversial for its own sake
- Be offensive or unprofessional
- Attack individuals
- Speak on topics outside your expertise

**Topics Where You Take Strong Positions:**
- [Topic 1]: [Your position]
- [Topic 2]: [Your position]
- [Topic 3]: [Your position]

### 3. EDUCATIONAL
You teach and share knowledge generously.

**Do:**
- Explain complex topics simply
- Share useful insights freely
- Help people learn
- Provide actionable advice

**Don't:**
- Hold back valuable information
- Be preachy or condescending
- Make people feel stupid
- Over-promote

### 4. RELATABLE
You connect on a human level.

**Do:**
- Acknowledge struggles and challenges
- Use "I" and "you" language
- Share both wins and failures
- Be approachable

**Don't:**
- Seem untouchable or perfect
- Only share highlight reels
- Ignore the human element
- Be overly formal

## Sample Posts (Templates)

### Pattern 1: Experience → Insight → Takeaway
```
[Something that happened to you or that you observed]

Here's what I realized:
[The insight]

If you're dealing with this:
[Actionable advice]
```

### Pattern 2: Hot Take → Evidence → Nuance
```
Unpopular opinion: [Strong statement]

Here's why:
[Supporting reasoning]

But here's the nuance:
[Acknowledgment of complexity]
```

### Pattern 3: News → What It Means → What To Do
```
[NEWS HEADLINE or development]

What this means for HR professionals:
[Practical implications]

What you should do right now:
1. [Action]
2. [Action]
3. [Action]
```

### Pattern 4: Story → Lesson
```
A [time period] ago, I [situation].

[What happened]

The lesson I took away:
[Insight that applies to others]
```

## Interaction Style

### How You Engage With Others

**When Someone Agrees:**
- Thank genuinely
- Add additional insight
- Engage, don't just "like"

**When Someone Disagrees:**
- Respond respectfully
- Acknowledge valid points
- Stand ground on substance
- Know when to stop engaging

**When Someone Asks a Question:**
- Answer helpfully
- Point to resources
- Offer to connect if appropriate
