## Positioning Statement

### Core Positioning

**IAML is the premier intensive employment law education provider for HR professionals, benefits specialists, and in-house counsel who need deep, practical legal knowledge taught by practicing attorneys—not conference-style overviews or academic theory.**

### Positioning Framework

| Element | IAML Position |
|---------|---------------|
| **For** | HR professionals, benefits specialists, and in-house counsel at companies with 250+ employees |
| **Who** | Need comprehensive employment law knowledge to protect their organizations and advance their careers |
| **IAML is** | An intensive, multi-day certificate program taught by practicing employment attorneys |
| **That provides** | Deep legal expertise, practical application, peer networking, and a prestigious credential |
| **Unlike** | SHRM conferences (broad, shallow sessions), law firm CLEs (brief overviews), or academic programs (theory-focused) |
| **IAML** | Delivers immersive learning from attorneys handling real cases today, with the peer networking and lasting credential that makes the investment worthwhile |

### The IAML Difference (Elevator Pitch)

> "For 45 years, IAML has trained over 80,000 HR professionals and attorneys through intensive, multi-day programs taught by practicing employment law attorneys—not academics or corporate trainers reading from textbooks. Our attendees don't just sit through sessions; they engage in real case discussions with peers from other organizations, building both expertise and professional networks that last throughout their careers. The IAML certificate isn't just a credential—it's evidence that your organization takes employment law seriously."

## Key Differentiators

### Differentiator 1: Practicing Attorneys, Not Academics

**What it means:**
IAML instructors are actively practicing employment law attorneys who handle real cases, conduct investigations, and advise clients daily. They teach from current experience, not outdated textbooks or theoretical frameworks.

**Why it matters:**
Employment law changes constantly—new NLRB rulings, evolving case law, updated regulations. Attorneys in active practice know what's happening right now, what strategies are working, and what mistakes are getting companies sued.

**Contrast with competitors:**
- SHRM conference sessions may include academics, consultants, or HR practitioners without legal expertise
- Law firm CLEs are taught by attorneys but typically cover narrow topics briefly
- Boutique providers often use less experienced instructors or those no longer in active practice

**Proof points:**
- Faculty roster includes partners at major employment law firms (Taft, Thompson Hine, Alston & Bird, etc.)
- Instructors share current case examples and recent rulings
- Multiple attorney perspectives in each program (not single-firm viewpoint)

---

### Differentiator 2: Intensive Multi-Day Immersion

**What it means:**
IAML programs are 2-4.5 days of focused, intensive education—not a 90-minute webinar or afternoon CLE session.

**Why it matters:**
Employment law is complex. You can't master workplace investigations, FMLA administration, or labor relations in an afternoon. Deep expertise requires sustained focus and comprehensive coverage.

**Contrast with competitors:**
- Law firm CLEs: Typically 1-3 hours, covering a single narrow topic
- SHRM conferences: Multiple short sessions (45-90 minutes each) across many topics; attendees choose from a menu
- Boutique providers: Often shorter programs with less depth

**Proof points:**
- 4.5-day programs include 29.75 SHRM/HRCI credits
- Comprehensive curriculum covering entire subject areas (not cherry-picked topics)
- Time for questions, discussion, and application

**The math:**
| Provider | Format | Hours of Instruction |
|----------|--------|---------------------|
| IAML (4.5 day) | Intensive program | ~35 hours on one subject |
| IAML (2 day) | Intensive program | ~15 hours on one subject |
| Law Firm CLE | Afternoon session | 2-3 hours |
| SHRM Conference | Multiple sessions | 4-6 hours across many topics |

---

### Differentiator 3: Peer Networking and Organic Learning

**What it means:**
IAML programs bring together HR professionals and counsel from different organizations who share challenges, solutions, and perspectives throughout the program.

**Why it matters:**
Some of the most valuable learning comes from hearing how peers at other companies handle similar issues. "How does your company handle intermittent FMLA leave?" "What's your investigation documentation process?" These conversations happen organically during IAML programs.

**Contrast with competitors:**
- Law firm CLEs: Usually one-way presentation; limited peer interaction
- Virtual programs: Networking is difficult or impossible
- Large conferences: Networking happens but is unfocused; conversations are brief

**How it works at IAML:**
- Group discussions during training where attendees share real situations
- Reception after the first day of each program
- Small enough cohorts for meaningful connection
- In-person format enables relationship building that lasts beyond the program

**Proof points:**
- Alumni testimonials frequently cite networking value
- Attendees return for additional programs partly to reconnect with IAML community
- Relationships formed at IAML programs lead to ongoing peer consultation

### Differentiator 4: The Prestigious IAML Certificate

**What it means:**
Graduates earn an IAML certificate that represents completion of comprehensive employment law training from a respected 45-year institution.

**Why it matters:**

**For individuals:**
- Career credential that signals expertise

**For organizations:**

**Proof points:**

- Graduates display certificates in offices

## Competitor Deep Dives

### SHRM (Society for Human Resource Management)

**What they offer:**
- Annual national conference (SHRM Annual Conference)
- Regional conferences and events
- SHRM-CP and SHRM-SCP certifications
- Online learning and resources
- Local chapter events

**Their strengths:**
- Dominant brand recognition in HR ("the place to be if you're in HR")
- Large-scale events with expo halls, keynote speakers, broad networking
- Certification pathway that many employers expect
- 340,000 members; 95% of Fortune 500 companies

**Their weaknesses:**
- Conference format = shallow coverage of many topics vs. deep dive on one
- Sessions are 45-90 minutes; attendees hop between topics
- Speakers vary widely in quality and expertise
- Employment law is one small slice of their broad HR focus
- Expensive when you add up registration + travel + hotel

**SHRM Pricing (2026 Annual Conference):**
- Member Early Bird: ~$2,095-$2,395
- Member Standard: ~$2,595
- Nonmember: ~$2,495-$2,795
- *Plus travel, hotel, meals*

**IAML vs. SHRM Talking Points:**

| Consideration | SHRM Conference | IAML Program |
|--------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Format | Many short sessions (choose your own) | Single intensive program (comprehensive coverage) |
| Depth | Surface-level on many topics | Deep expertise on one subject area |
| Instructors | Varies (practitioners, consultants, academics) | Practicing employment law attorneys |
| Networking | Large crowds, brief interactions | Smaller cohorts, meaningful relationships |
| Credential | Conference attendance | Prestigious certificate |
| Focus | Broad HR topics | Employment law expertise |
| Total cost | Registration + travel + hotel + time | Registration + travel + hotel + time |

**When someone chooses SHRM over IAML:**
- They want the "conference experience" and broad exposure
- Brand recognition ("everyone goes to SHRM")
- They're early in their HR career and want general exposure
- Their company has a relationship with SHRM or mandates SHRM certification

**When IAML wins over SHRM:**
- They need deep employment law expertise, not broad HR exposure
- They've been to SHRM conferences and want something more substantive
- They value learning from practicing attorneys
- They want the networking depth that comes from multi-day immersion with a cohort

## Objection Handling

### Objection: "It's expensive"

**Understand the objection:**
The $2,375 program fee isn't usually the issue—it's competitive with SHRM conferences. The real cost concern is typically:
- Travel and hotel expenses for in-person programs
- Time away from the office (4.5 days)
- Needing to justify the investment to a supervisor

**Response framework:**

**Reframe the investment:**
> "When you factor in what you're getting—35+ hours of instruction from practicing employment law attorneys, peer networking, comprehensive materials, and a credential that demonstrates your expertise—the investment per hour of training is remarkably reasonable."

**Address the real cost (litigation):**
> "Consider what a single employment lawsuit costs your organization—not just legal fees, but management time, morale impact, and potential settlement. One situation handled correctly because of what you learned at IAML pays for the program many times over."

**Emphasize the time value:**
> "Your time is valuable. That's exactly why we've designed intensive multi-day programs rather than spreading training across months of webinars. You invest focused time once and leave with comprehensive expertise you can apply immediately."

**For the supervisor justification:**
> "We can provide documentation of learning outcomes and CE credits that demonstrates the professional development value. Many of our attendees find that emphasizing the risk mitigation and litigation defense aspects resonates with leadership."

---

### Objection: "I can't be away from the office for 4.5 days"

**Understand the objection:**
This is often the biggest barrier, especially for stretched-thin HR teams.

**Response framework:**

**Acknowledge the challenge:**
> "I understand—being away for nearly a week is a real commitment, especially when your team is already stretched."

**Reframe the time investment:**
> "Here's how our most successful attendees think about it: this is 4.5 days that will make you dramatically more effective for the next several years. The depth of knowledge you gain simply isn't possible in a series of one-hour webinars—you'd spend more total time and learn less."

**Offer alternatives:**
> "If the full program isn't feasible right now, you could start with a single block on the topic most pressing for you. Many attendees complete their certificate over time by attending different blocks."

**Highlight the destination locations:**
> "We intentionally hold programs in cities worth visiting—Denver, Scottsdale, Newport Beach, Atlanta, Washington D.C. Some attendees extend their trip by a day to enjoy the location."

---

### Objection: "Can't I just Google this / use AI / find it online?"

**Understand the objection:**
This reflects a misunderstanding of employment law complexity and risk.

**Response framework:**

**Acknowledge partial truth:**
> "You can certainly find employment law information online, and AI tools are getting better at providing general guidance."

**Highlight the risk:**
> "But here's the challenge: employment law is incredibly nuanced and varies by jurisdiction, company size, industry, and specific circumstances. Generic information can be dangerously misleading. When you're deciding whether to terminate an employee, how to respond to a union organizing campaign, or how to handle an ADA accommodation request, the cost of getting it wrong is enormous—lawsuits, EEOC charges, NLRB complaints."

**Emphasize what can't be Googled:**
> "More importantly, you can't Google the judgment that comes from practicing attorneys sharing how they've handled similar situations, or the peer insights from HR professionals at other companies facing the same challenges. That experiential knowledge is what makes IAML training so valuable."

---

### Objection: "Why not just go to SHRM?"

**Understand the objection:**
SHRM has dominant brand recognition. Many HR professionals assume SHRM is "the" place for HR education.

**Response framework:**

**Acknowledge SHRM's value:**
> "SHRM conferences are great for broad HR exposure and networking across the profession. Many of our attendees also attend SHRM events."

**Differentiate the purpose:**
> "IAML serves a different need. When you need deep expertise in employment law—not a 60-minute overview, but comprehensive knowledge you can apply to complex situations—that's what we deliver. Our programs are 4.5 days focused entirely on one subject area, taught by practicing attorneys who handle these issues daily."

**Use the comparison:**
> "Think of it this way: SHRM is a buffet with many small plates. IAML is a multi-course meal from a master chef, focused on one cuisine. Both have value—it depends on what you're hungry for."

---

### Objection: "Our outside counsel provides free CLEs"

**Understand the objection:**
In-house counsel especially may feel they're already getting adequate legal education from their law firm relationships.

**Response framework:**

**Acknowledge the value:**
> "Free CLEs from your outside counsel are a great benefit, and you should absolutely take advantage of them."

**Differentiate the depth:**
> "What we hear from in-house counsel who attend IAML is that law firm CLEs are great for staying current on narrow topics, but they're typically 2-3 hours covering one specific issue. IAML programs provide comprehensive coverage—the full landscape of employment law or benefits law—in a way that's simply not possible in an afternoon session."

**Add the perspective angle:**
> "You're also getting one firm's perspective at a law firm CLE. At IAML, you learn from multiple attorneys at different firms, plus you hear how HR professionals and counsel at other companies are handling similar challenges. That diversity of perspective is incredibly valuable."

## Win/Loss Patterns

### When IAML Wins

**Common winning scenarios:**
- Prospect has attended SHRM conferences and wants something deeper
- Organization has had employment litigation and wants better training
- HR professional is serious about career advancement
- Company values demonstrated compliance investment
- Prospect researches faculty credentials and program quality
- Word-of-mouth referral from IAML alumnus

**What winners say:**
- "I wanted real depth, not another conference with surface-level sessions"
- "The practicing attorney angle was the deciding factor"
- "A colleague who attended recommended it highly"
- "We needed to show we take employment law training seriously"

### When IAML Loses

**Common losing scenarios:**
- Prospect defaults to SHRM due to brand recognition
- Budget constraints push them to cheaper alternatives
- Time constraints make 4.5 days impossible
- Prospect doesn't know IAML exists (awareness gap)
- Decision-maker values "checking the box" over quality

**What losers typically choose instead:**
- SHRM conference (brand recognition, "the place to be")
- Law firm CLEs (free, convenient)
- Nothing (training deprioritized)
- Cheaper boutique provider

### Strategic Implications

**To win more:**
1. Build awareness among HR professionals who don't know IAML
2. Offer entry points (free quarterly updates, single blocks) to demonstrate quality
3. Emphasize depth vs. breadth comparison with SHRM
4. Leverage alumni testimonials and referrals
5. Position the litigation defense / risk mitigation value for budget justification
