## Marketing Mission

### Purpose

Build IAML's market presence and registration pipeline through intelligent, data-driven marketing that demonstrates the unique value of intensive employment law education from practicing attorneys.

### Strategic Principles

1. **Intelligence-First**: Every campaign generates data that improves future campaigns
2. **Quality Over Volume**: Better to reach the right 150 targets than blast 10,000 wrong ones
3. **Relationship Building**: Marketing opens doors; relationships close deals
4. **Prove Value First**: Free quarterly updates and block attendance demonstrate quality before asking for full investment
5. **Multichannel Coordination**: Email, LinkedIn, and direct outreach work together, not in silos

## Strategic Initiatives

### Initiative 1: Alumni Re-Engagement Campaign

**Priority**: HIGH - Quick Win
**Timeline**: Q1 2026

**Objective**: Re-activate 3,500 past participants by offering complimentary 12-month access to Quarterly Employment Law Updates—an alumni benefit that rebuilds relationships and creates opportunities for repeat attendance and colleague referrals.

**The Offer:**
- **ALL past participants** receive complimentary 12-month Quarterly Employment Law Update access
- This is an alumni benefit, not a special promotion—it applies regardless of title/seniority
- Future registrants will also receive this as part of their program value

**Why This Works:**
- Re-establishes contact with dormant alumni
- Demonstrates ongoing value of IAML relationship
- Creates regular touchpoints (quarterly) for future marketing
- Senior alumni may send team members to paid programs
- All alumni may refer colleagues or return for additional programs

**Approach:**
1. Email all 3,500 alumni announcing complimentary quarterly update access
2. Track who engages (opens, clicks, attends quarterly updates)
3. Follow-up sequences based on engagement:
   - Engaged senior alumni → corporate training conversations, team development
   - Engaged other alumni → program recommendations, colleague referral incentives
4. Colleague referral campaign (invitationtohrtraining.com domains)

**Success Metrics:**
- Alumni re-engagement rate (open, click, signup)
- Quarterly update attendance from alumni
- Alumni conversions to additional paid programs
- Colleague referrals generated

**Email Domain Strategy**: Use IAML-branded domains (8 mailboxes) for alumni communication—they know us.

### Initiative 2: ABM Target Pipeline (Senior HR at High-Value Companies)

**Priority**: HIGH - Core Growth Engine
**Timeline**: Ongoing, starting Q1 2026

**Objective**: Identify and engage 150 high-value target accounts monthly through multichannel outreach, using a two-stage free offer progression to build relationships with senior HR leaders who can send their teams to IAML programs.

**Who Gets Free Offers (New Prospects Only):**
- **VP, SVP, CHRO, Chief People Officer, HR Director** at targeted companies
- Must be NEW prospects (not past participants—they already get alumni benefits)
- These are decision-makers who can send their teams to IAML programs

**Two-Stage Free Offer Progression:**

*Stage 1: Free Quarterly Employment Law Update*
- Initial offer to senior HR at targeted companies
- Low commitment, demonstrates IAML quality
- Goal: Get them to experience our faculty and content

*Stage 2: Free Virtual Training (If They Engage)*
- Offered after they attend/engage with quarterly update
- Can be for themselves OR one of their team members
- Full block or program experience
- Goal: Deeper demonstration of IAML value, relationship building

**Who Does NOT Get Free Offers:**
- HR Managers, Generalists, entry-level HR at targeted companies
- Anyone not on the targeted lists or select companies
- These roles receive standard marketing (promotional emails, program information)
- They can register and pay like normal prospects

**Approach:**
1. Monthly workflow identifies 150 high-value target companies from database
2. Identify senior HR leadership (VP+) at each target company
3. Multichannel outreach sequence to senior leaders:
   - LinkedIn connection request (personalized)
   - Email introduction with free Quarterly Update offer
   - Follow-up touchpoints
   - Phone outreach for highest-priority targets (as needed)
4. Track engagement with quarterly update
5. Engaged senior leaders → Offer free virtual training for them or team member
6. Post-training: Relationship building, understand team development needs
7. Position IAML as partner for developing their entire HR organization

**Success Metrics:**
- LinkedIn connection acceptance rate
- Email response rate
- Stage 1: Quarterly update signups from senior HR (target: 25% = ~38/month)
- Stage 2: Free virtual training conversions from engaged prospects
- Ultimate: Team member registrations from engaged senior leaders
- Corporate training conversations initiated

**Email Domain Strategy**: Use general marketing domains (Google Workspace) for cold outreach to new prospects.

### Current Infrastructure

**Google Workspace (30 mailboxes):**

- Use for: ABM campaigns, local campaigns, general prospecting

**Microsoft 365 (30 mailboxes):**

| Domain Type | Mailboxes | Use Case |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| SHRM-referenced | 4 | Target SHRM-CP/SCP holders, emphasize SHRM credits |
| HRCI-referenced | 4 | Target PHR/SPHR holders, emphasize HRCI credits |
| IAML-branded | 8 | Alumni communication, known contacts |
| invitationtohrtraining.com | 4 | Colleague referral campaigns (discounts for colleagues of registrants) |
| Other | 10 | Various campaigns, expansion |

### Email Sending Strategy

**Guiding Principle**: Protect domain reputation. Better to send less and maintain deliverability than burn domains with high volume.

**Recommended Daily Limits (Conservative):**
- New/cold domains: 20-30 emails per mailbox per day
- Established domains with good reputation: 50-75 emails per mailbox per day
- Warm lists (alumni, engaged contacts): Can send more frequently

**Current Capacity (Conservative):**
- 60 mailboxes × 25 emails/day = 1,500 emails/day capacity
- Monthly capacity: ~30,000-45,000 emails

**Scaling Strategy:**
1. Start conservative (20-25/mailbox/day)
2. Monitor deliverability and engagement metrics
3. Gradually increase volume on high-performing domains
4. Add new domains/mailboxes when:
   - Consistent high engagement on existing domains
   - Campaign needs exceed current capacity
   - Want to segment campaigns further

**Domain Acquisition Criteria (Future):**
- Relevant to HR/training/employment law
- Clean history (check blacklists)
- Aged domains preferred over brand new
- Consider: certificate, compliance, workplace, employer, etc.

| **Software/Tools** | GHL, data providers, scraping tools | Spreadsheet |

| **Events/Partnerships** | SHRM chapter fees, sponsorships (future) | Spreadsheet |
| **Time Investment** | Hours spent on marketing activities | Optional tracking |

### ROI Calculation Framework

```
Registration Value = Program Fee ($1,575 or $2,375)

Cost Per Lead = Total Marketing Spend / Total Leads Generated

Cost Per Registration = Total Marketing Spend / Total Registrations

Marketing ROI = (Registration Revenue - Marketing Spend) / Marketing Spend

Channel ROI = (Channel Revenue - Channel Spend) / Channel Spend
```

### Future Paid Advertising Approach

**Trigger for Paid**: When organic channels are working and we want to scale faster

**Likely First Paid Channels:**
1. LinkedIn Ads (highly targeted to HR titles)
2. Google Ads (employment law training searches)

**Paid Budget Philosophy**: Start small, test, prove ROI, then scale
