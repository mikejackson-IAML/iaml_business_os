## Overview

### Purpose of LinkedIn Outreach

LinkedIn outreach complements email in the multichannel ABM approach:

- **Humanizes the outreach**: They see your face, your content, your network
- **Builds persistent connection**: Once connected, you're in their feed forever
- **Higher trust signal**: Connection implies mutual recognition
- **Enables ongoing nurture**: Your organic content reaches them automatically

### Key Principles

- **Personalization is non-negotiable**: Generic requests get ignored
- **Value before ask**: Build relationship before pitching
- **Respect the platform**: LinkedIn is professional; don't be salesy or spammy
- **Play the long game**: Connection today, conversion later
- **Coordinate with email**: LinkedIn and email work together, not separately

### Who to Target via LinkedIn

| Priority | Audience | Goal |
|----------|----------|------|
| 1 | Senior HR at ABM target companies | Free quarterly update → relationship → team training |
| 2 | HR leaders who engage with IAML content | Capitalize on warm signals |
| 3 | HR professionals in program cities | Geo-targeted awareness |
| 4 | In-house employment counsel | CLE + training opportunity |

## Outreach Strategy

### Daily/Weekly Cadence

| Activity | Daily Target | Weekly Target |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| Connection requests sent | 10-15 | 50-75 |
| DMs to new connections | As they accept | 20-30 |
| Follow-up DMs | 5-10 | 25-50 |
| Engagement on targets' content | 5-10 comments | 25-50 |

**Note**: LinkedIn has connection request limits. Stay under 100/week to avoid restrictions.

### Multichannel Timing with Email

| Day | LinkedIn Activity | Email Activity |
|-----|-------------------|----------------|
| Day 1 | Connection request sent | — |
| Day 3 | — | Email 1 sent |
| Day 3-7 | If connected: Thank you DM | — |
| Day 6 | — | Email 2 sent |
| Day 7-10 | If not connected: Engage with their content | — |
| Day 10 | — | Email 3 sent |
| Day 14+ | Continue engaging, DM if connected | Email 4-5 |

### Warm-Up Before Outreach

Before sending a connection request, consider "warming up" the target:

1. **View their profile** (they'll see the notification)
2. **Like one of their posts** (appears in their notifications)
3. **Comment on their post** (stronger signal)
4. **Wait 1-2 days**, then send connection request

This creates a "Oh, I've seen this person" moment when they receive your request.

## Connection Request Templates

### Character Limit

LinkedIn connection notes have a **300 character limit**. Be concise.

### Template 1: Direct Introduction (ABM Targets)

**Use when**: First touch to senior HR at target companies

```
Hi [First Name], I lead IAML—we train HR teams on employment law with practicing attorneys. Given your role at [Company], thought it'd be valuable to connect. Always happy to share insights on [relevant topic].
```

**Character count**: ~250

---

### Template 2: Shared Interest/Context

**Use when**: You can reference something specific (their post, shared connection, industry)

```
Hi [First Name], saw your post on [topic]—great point about [specific thing]. I run IAML (employment law training for HR) and we see that issue come up a lot. Would love to connect and share thoughts.
```

**Character count**: ~240

---

### Template 3: Geographic Relevance

**Use when**: They're in a city where you're running a program

```
Hi [First Name], noticed you're in [City]. We're bringing IAML's employment law training there in [Month]—thought it'd be good to connect with local HR leaders. Would love to be a resource.
```

**Character count**: ~230

---

### Template 4: Credential-Based

**Use when**: They have SHRM/HRCI credentials visible

```
Hi [First Name], fellow HR professional here. I run IAML—we offer SHRM/HRCI-accredited employment law programs taught by practicing attorneys. Always looking to connect with [SHRM-CP/SPHR/etc.] professionals.
```

**Character count**: ~240

---

### Template 5: Soft/Curious (Lower Pressure)

**Use when**: You want a lighter touch

```
Hi [First Name], your work at [Company] caught my attention. I'm in the HR training space (employment law focus) and always interested in connecting with HR leaders. Hope you're open to it.
```

**Character count**: ~220

---

### Template 6: Mutual Connection

**Use when**: You share a connection

```
Hi [First Name], noticed we're both connected to [Mutual Connection]. I lead IAML (employment law training for HR teams) and thought it'd be good to connect directly. Hope you're open to it.
```

**Character count**: ~230

---

### What NOT to Do

**Avoid these in connection requests:**

❌ Pitching your product/program immediately
❌ Long paragraphs (they won't read)
❌ Generic templates that don't feel personalized
❌ "I'd love to pick your brain" (feels extractive)
❌ Asking for a call in the connection request
❌ Mentioning competitors

## DM Sequence (Post-Connection)

### When They Accept Your Connection

**Timing**: Within 24-48 hours of acceptance

### DM 1: Thank You + Soft Open

**Goal**: Acknowledge the connection, offer value, no hard ask

```
Thanks for connecting, [First Name]!

I noticed you're leading HR at [Company]—always great to connect with someone navigating [relevant challenge: employment law, growing teams, compliance, etc.].

If you ever want to chat about HR training or just swap notes on what you're seeing in the market, I'm around. No agenda—just happy to be connected.
```

**Alternative (shorter):**

```
Appreciate the connection, [First Name]. If IAML can ever be a resource for you or your team on employment law, just let me know. Happy to share insights anytime.
```

---

### DM 2: Value Offer (3-5 Days After DM 1)

**Goal**: Introduce the quarterly update offer (senior HR only)

**Only send if**: They responded positively to DM 1, OR they've engaged with your content

```
Hey [First Name], quick thought—

We run quarterly employment law updates (90 min, covers latest NLRB rulings, discrimination developments, etc.) taught by practicing attorneys.

I'd be happy to give you complimentary access to the next one ([Date]). No pitch, just a way to see what IAML does.

Interested?
```

**If they haven't responded to DM 1:**

Wait. Don't rapid-fire DMs. Continue engaging with their content and wait for a warmer signal.

---

### DM 3: Follow-Up on Value Offer (5-7 Days After DM 2)

**Only send if**: They didn't respond to DM 2 but are still engaging with your content

```
Hey [First Name], just bumping this up. The quarterly update on [topic] is coming up [date].

Happy to send you access if you're interested—no strings attached.

Either way, hope you're having a good week.
```

---

### DM 4: Content Share (Alternative to Hard Push)

**Use if**: They're not responding but you want to stay warm

```
Thought you'd find this interesting, [First Name]: [Share a LinkedIn post, article, or insight relevant to them]

[One line on why it's relevant to their role/company]

Let me know what you think.
```

## Follow-Up Strategies

### For Non-Responders (Connected but Silent)

**Strategy**: Stay visible through organic engagement, don't keep pushing via DM

1. **Engage with their content** (like, comment) consistently
2. **Post valuable content** they'll see in their feed
3. **Wait 30-60 days** before another DM attempt
4. **Re-approach with new angle** (new quarterly update topic, relevant news, program announcement)

### For Warm Responders (Interested but Not Acting)

**Strategy**: Gentle persistence with value

1. **Acknowledge their interest** without pressure
2. **Make it easy** to say yes (send direct registration links, offer to handle details)
3. **Set a soft deadline** if there's a real one ("The session is next week—happy to hold a spot")
4. **Follow up once more** if they go silent

### For Hot Responders (Ready to Act)

**Strategy**: Move quickly, make it frictionless

1. **Respond within hours**
2. **Send registration link or next step immediately**
3. **Confirm receipt and set expectations**
4. **Transition to email for logistics** (easier to track)

## Response Handling

### Response: Positive Interest

**They say**: "Yes, I'd like access" / "Sounds interesting" / "Tell me more"

**Your response:**

```
Great to hear, [First Name]!

Here's the registration link: [link]

The session is [date] at [time], and we'll cover [brief topic overview]. You'll receive a calendar invite once you register.

Looking forward to having you there. Let me know if you have any questions.
```

---

### Response: Soft Interest / Maybe

**They say**: "Interesting, maybe" / "Not now but maybe later" / "I'll think about it"

**Your response:**

```
Totally understand, [First Name]. No pressure at all.

I'll drop you a note when the next session is scheduled—you can decide then if timing works better.

In the meantime, feel free to reach out if you have any questions about HR training for your team.
```

---

### Response: Questions About IAML

**They say**: "What exactly does IAML do?" / "Tell me about your programs"

**Your response:**

```
Happy to share!

IAML has been training HR professionals for 45 years. We run intensive certificate programs (2-4.5 days) taught by practicing employment attorneys—not academics or consultants.

Our most popular program is the Certificate in Employee Relations Law, which covers hiring to termination, discrimination defense, ADA/FMLA, and more.

What's your team's biggest training need right now? I can point you to what's most relevant.
```

---

### Response: Referral to Someone Else

**They say**: "I don't handle this, but [Name] does" / "You should talk to our L&D team"

**Your response:**

```
Really appreciate the redirect, [First Name].

If you're comfortable, could you share their contact or make an intro? Happy to reach out directly and mention you connected us.

Either way, thanks for taking the time.
```

---

### Response: Not Interested

**They say**: "Not interested" / "Please stop" / "We're all set"

**Your response:**

```
Totally understood, [First Name]. I'll make a note and won't reach out again.

If anything changes down the road, the door's always open. Wishing you and the team all the best.
```

**Action**: Remove from outreach sequences. Do not contact again unless they initiate.

---

### Response: Negative/Hostile

**They say**: Something rude or aggressive

**Your response:**

```
Apologies if my message came across wrong—that wasn't my intention. I'll remove you from future outreach. Best of luck.
```

**Action**: Remove immediately. Do not engage further.

## InMail Strategy

### When to Use InMail

InMail (paid LinkedIn messages to non-connections) should be used sparingly:

- **High-value targets** who haven't accepted connection requests
- **Time-sensitive opportunities** (e.g., they're in a program city soon)
- **Re-engagement** of previously connected contacts who disconnected

### InMail Best Practices

- **Subject line matters**: Keep it short, relevant, not salesy
- **Even shorter than regular DM**: They didn't opt into hearing from you
- **Value-first**: Lead with what you're offering, not asking
- **Single CTA**: One clear ask

### InMail Template

**Subject**: Quick resource for [Company]'s HR team

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

I tried connecting earlier but wanted to make sure this reached you.

I lead IAML—we train HR teams on employment law with practicing attorneys. I'd like to offer you complimentary access to our next Quarterly Employment Law Update ([date]) covering [topic].

No pitch, just a way to see what we do. Worth 90 minutes?

Either way, happy to connect if you're open to it.

— Mike
```

## Coordination with Email

### Parallel Tracking

For each ABM target, track both LinkedIn and email status:

| Field | What to Track |
|-------|---------------|
| LinkedIn Connection Status | Not sent / Pending / Connected / Declined |
| LinkedIn DM Status | Not sent / Sent (date) / Responded (date) |
| Email Status | Sequence stage (1-5) / Responded / Unsubscribed |
| Overall Status | Cold / Engaged / Qualified / Converted |

### Coordinating Messaging

**If they respond on LinkedIn:**
- Note in CRM
- Can reference email: "I also sent you an email about this—happy to continue either here or there"
- Tailor email follow-ups to reference LinkedIn conversation

**If they respond to email:**
- Pause aggressive LinkedIn DMs
- Continue organic engagement (likes, comments)
- Can reference in DM: "Saw your email reply—thanks! Happy to chat here too"

**If they respond to neither:**
- Continue both channels until sequence complete
- Prioritize channel where they're more active (check post frequency, engagement patterns)

## Tracking & Metrics

### What to Track

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| Connection request acceptance rate | How compelling are your requests? |
| DM response rate | How engaging is your messaging? |
| Quarterly update signups from LinkedIn | Conversion effectiveness |
| Time to response | How "warm" are your targets? |

### Benchmarks

| Metric | Good | Great |
|--------|------|-------|
| Connection acceptance rate | 30% | 50%+ |
| DM response rate | 15% | 30%+ |
| Quarterly update conversion (from DM offer) | 10% | 25%+ |

### Weekly Review

1. How many connection requests sent vs. accepted?
2. How many DM conversations started?
3. How many quarterly update signups from LinkedIn?
4. Any patterns in what's working/not working?

## Tools & Efficiency

### LinkedIn Sales Navigator

**Recommended for**: Finding targets, tracking engagement, building lists

**Key features to use:**
- Advanced search (title, company size, geography)
- Lead lists for ABM targets
- Activity alerts (see when targets post or change jobs)
- InMail credits

### Efficiency Tips

1. **Batch connection requests**: Do 10-15 at once, same time each day
2. **Template personalization**: Have templates ready, customize 1-2 elements
3. **Use voice notes** (optional): LinkedIn voice notes can stand out—use for high-priority targets
4. **Save responses**: Build a library of response templates for common situations
