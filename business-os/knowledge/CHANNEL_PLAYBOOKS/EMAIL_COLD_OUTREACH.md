## Overview

### Purpose of Cold Outreach

Cold email serves two distinct purposes for IAML:

1. **ABM (Senior HR)**: Build relationships with VP+ HR leaders at target companies → free quarterly update → free virtual training → paid programs for their teams
2. **General Prospecting**: Drive registrations from HR professionals who aren't in the ABM funnel

### Key Principles

- **Value first**: Lead with what they get, not what you're selling
- **Personalization matters**: Generic emails get ignored
- **Respect their time**: Keep emails scannable and concise
- **Never salesy**: IAML's voice is confident and helpful, not pushy
- **Compliance**: Always include unsubscribe option, honor opt-outs immediately

## Email Infrastructure Rules

### Domain Usage

| Audience | Domains to Use | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| New cold prospects | Google Workspace (general marketing domains) | Protects IAML brand domains |
| SHRM credential holders | Microsoft 365 (SHRM-referenced domains) | Relevance increases open rates |
| HRCI credential holders | Microsoft 365 (HRCI-referenced domains) | Relevance increases open rates |
| Colleague referrals | invitationtohrtraining.com | Distinct from main prospecting |

### Sending Limits

**Protect domain reputation above all else.**

| Domain Status | Daily Limit per Mailbox | Notes |
|---------------|------------------------|-------|
| New/establishing | 20-25 emails | First 30 days |
| Established, good reputation | 30-50 emails | Monitor deliverability |
| High-performing | 50-75 emails | Only if metrics stay strong |

**Warning signs to reduce volume:**
- Open rates dropping below 15%
- Bounce rates above 3%
- Spam complaints
- Emails landing in spam (test regularly)

### Sending Schedule

**Best days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
**Best times**: 9:00-11:00 AM recipient's local time, or 1:00-3:00 PM
**Avoid**: Monday morning, Friday afternoon, weekends

## ABM Sequence (Senior HR at Target Companies)

### Audience

- VP of HR, SVP of HR, CHRO, Chief People Officer, HR Director
- At companies from monthly 150 target list
- NEW prospects only (not past participants)

### Offer

**Stage 1**: Complimentary Quarterly Employment Law Update
**Stage 2** (if engaged): Free virtual training for them or a team member

### Sequence Structure

**5 emails over 21 days**, coordinated with LinkedIn outreach

| Email | Day | Focus | Goal |
|-------|-----|-------|------|
| 1 | Day 3* | Value introduction | Open, click, or reply |
| 2 | Day 6 | Different angle | Catch those who missed #1 |
| 3 | Day 10 | Social proof | Build credibility |
| 4 | Day 15 | Content/value share | Provide value even if no response |
| 5 | Day 21 | Breakup | Final response opportunity |

*Day 1 is LinkedIn connection request; Email 1 goes Day 3

## Re-Engagement Sequence

### Audience

- Prospects who completed a sequence without responding
- Wait 60-90 days before re-engaging

### Sequence Structure

**3 emails over 10 days**

Fresh angle required—don't repeat the original sequence.

### Re-Engagement Angles

Pick one angle per re-engagement attempt:

1. **New quarterly update topic**: "Our upcoming session covers [new timely topic]"
2. **Relevant news/ruling**: "Given [recent NLRB decision/lawsuit/regulation], thought you'd want to know..."
3. **Program announcement**: "We just announced our [season] schedule, including [city] in [month]"
4. **Content share**: Share a valuable insight with no ask, then soft follow-up
5. **Different program focus**: If original was about ERL, try Benefits or Investigations angle

### Template: News-Based Re-Engagement

**Email 1:**

```
Hi [First Name],

I reached out a few months ago about IAML's employment law training. A lot has changed since then—including some significant [NLRB rulings / regulatory updates / court decisions] that affect HR teams directly.

[2-3 sentence summary of the relevant development and its implications]

Our next Quarterly Employment Law Update covers this and other recent developments. Given your role at [Company], I thought you might find it valuable.

Interested in complimentary access? Just reply.

[Signature]
```

**Email 2 (Day 4):**

## Response Handling

### Positive Response (Interested)

**Immediate actions:**
1. Reply within 4 hours (same day if possible)
2. Send quarterly update registration link
3. Add to "Engaged ABM" list in Supabase
4. Note in CRM: interested, date, context

**Follow-up sequence:**
- After they register: Confirmation + what to expect
- After they attend: Thank you + feedback request + Stage 2 offer (free virtual training)
- Ongoing: Monthly value emails, program announcements

**Template: Response to Interest**

```
Hi [First Name],

Great to hear from you! I'm glad the quarterly update sounds valuable.

Here's the registration link: [link]

The session is [date] at [time]. It's 90 minutes with our faculty covering [brief topic overview]. You'll receive a calendar invite and reminder before the session.

Looking forward to having you there. And if you have any questions beforehand, just reply to this email.

[Signature]
```

---

### Soft Positive (Maybe Later / Not Now)

**Actions:**
1. Acknowledge and respect timing
2. Add to "Nurture" list
3. Set reminder to follow up in 60-90 days

**Template:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Completely understand—timing is everything. I'll make a note to check back in a few months.

In the meantime, if you'd like to stay loosely connected, I share occasional insights on LinkedIn: [profile link]

Wishing you and the team at [Company] all the best until then.

[Signature]
```

### Referral (Someone Else Handles This)

**Actions:**
1. Thank them
2. Get the referral name/contact if possible
3. Reach out to the referral (mention the connection)

**Template:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Thanks so much for pointing me in the right direction. I appreciate it.

If you're comfortable sharing [Name]'s email, I'll reach out directly and mention you connected us. If you'd prefer to forward my note to them, that works too.

Either way, thanks for taking the time.

[Signature]
```

### Negative Response (Not Interested / Unsubscribe)

**Actions:**
1. Respond graciously
2. Remove from all outreach immediately
3. Add to suppression list

**Template:**

```

[Signature]
```

## A/B Testing Framework

### What to Test

| Element | Test Options |
|---------|--------------|
| Subject lines | Question vs. statement, personalization vs. none, length |
| Opening line | Personalized vs. generic, pain point vs. value |
| Email length | Short (under 100 words) vs. medium (100-200) |
| CTA | Reply vs. click link, specific vs. general |
| Send time | Morning vs. afternoon |
| Send day | Tuesday vs. Wednesday vs. Thursday |

### How to Test

1. **Split your list evenly** for each test
2. **Test one variable at a time**
3. **Minimum sample size**: 100 per variation
4. **Wait for results**: At least 48 hours before measuring opens, 1 week for replies
5. **Document results**: Track what works and update templates

### Metrics to Track

| Metric | Good | Great | Investigate If Below |
|--------|------|-------|---------------------|
| Open rate | 25% | 35%+ | 15% |
| Click rate | 3% | 5%+ | 1% |
| Reply rate | 2% | 5%+ | 0.5% |
| Bounce rate | Under 2% | Under 1% | Above 3% |
