## Overview

### Purpose of Local Campaigns

Local campaigns drive registrations for in-person programs by targeting HR professionals in the program city and surrounding metro area.

### Why Local Matters

- **Reduced friction**: No flights, less expense, easier approval
- **Same-day travel**: Some can drive in for the day
- **Local relevance**: "IAML is coming to [your city]" creates urgency
- **Untapped audience**: People who wouldn't consider traveling may attend locally

### Campaign Scope

**2026 Program Cities**: 9 cities, 2-4 programs each
- *Specific cities to be added from program schedule*

**Target Audience**:
- HR professionals at companies in the metro area
- All company sizes (wider net than ABM—not limited to 250+)
- All HR titles (not just senior—local has lower barrier)

## Campaign Timeline

### Extended Timeline (3-4 Months Out)

Higher-ticket programs ($2,375) require advance planning:
- Budget approval takes time
- Time off requires coordination
- Travel arrangements (even local) need booking

### Timeline Overview

| Weeks Out | Phase | Key Activities |
|-----------|-------|----------------|
| 12-16 | **Preparation** | Compile local data, plan campaign, prepare assets |
| 10-12 | **Launch** | Begin email campaign, start LinkedIn outreach |
| 8-10 | **Awareness** | Continue emails, LinkedIn content featuring city |
| 6-8 | **Engagement** | Second wave emails, virtual option introduced |
| 4-6 | **Conversion** | Urgency messaging, final push |
| 2-4 | **Close** | Last chance emails, campaign winds down |
| 0-2 | **Pre-program** | Campaign ends, shift to attendee communication |

### Detailed Week-by-Week

**Weeks 12-16: Preparation**
- [ ] Compile local HR professional list (LinkedIn Sales Navigator)
- [ ] Enrich data with emails where possible
- [ ] Segment list by title/seniority
- [ ] Prepare email templates (customize for city)
- [ ] Plan LinkedIn content calendar with city references
- [ ] Identify local SHRM chapter (for future partnership)

**Weeks 10-12: Launch**
- [ ] Send Email 1 (Program announcement)
- [ ] Begin LinkedIn connection requests to local HR leaders
- [ ] Post LinkedIn content mentioning upcoming program
- [ ] Track initial engagement

**Weeks 8-10: Awareness Building**
- [ ] Send Email 2 (Value/curriculum focus)
- [ ] Continue LinkedIn outreach
- [ ] Engage with local HR professionals' content
- [ ] Monitor and respond to inquiries

**Weeks 6-8: Virtual Introduction**
- [ ] Send Email 3 (Introduce virtual option)
- [ ] LinkedIn post about virtual availability
- [ ] Follow up with engaged non-converters

**Weeks 4-6: Conversion Push**
- [ ] Send Email 4 (Urgency + social proof)
- [ ] Direct outreach to highly engaged prospects
- [ ] Phone calls to warm leads (optional)

**Weeks 2-4: Final Close**
- [ ] Send Email 5 (Last chance)
- [ ] Final LinkedIn push
- [ ] Campaign ends at 2 weeks out

**Why stop at 2 weeks:**
- People need lead time for travel/approval
- Last-minute registrations are rare for this price point
- Shifts focus to attendee preparation

## Data Gathering & List Building

### Primary Data Source

**LinkedIn Sales Navigator** (metro area selection)

### Search Criteria

| Filter | Setting |
|--------|---------|
| Geography | [City] Metro Area |
| Title | HR Director, VP HR, CHRO, HR Manager, Employee Relations, etc. |
| Company Size | All (wider net for local) |
| Industry | All (unless specific industry focus) |

### Title Variations to Include

**Senior (prioritize):**
- Chief Human Resources Officer
- Chief People Officer
- VP Human Resources
- Vice President HR
- SVP Human Resources
- HR Director
- Director of Human Resources
- Director of Employee Relations

**Mid-Level:**
- Senior HR Manager
- HR Manager
- Employee Relations Manager
- HR Business Partner
- Senior HR Business Partner

**Generalist (include but lower priority):**
- HR Generalist
- Senior HR Generalist
- HR Specialist

**Benefits (for benefits programs):**
- Benefits Director
- Benefits Manager
- Compensation and Benefits Manager
- Total Rewards Manager

### Data Enrichment

For prospects without email:
- LinkedIn outreach only
- Use tools like Hunter.io, Apollo, or similar to find company emails
- Check company websites for HR team pages

### List Segmentation

| Segment | Criteria | Message Focus |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| **Senior Local** | VP+, Director | Decision-maker messaging, team development angle |
| **Mid Local** | Manager, HRBP | Career development, practical skills |
| **Generalist Local** | Generalist, Specialist | Foundational training, certification |

## LinkedIn Local Strategy

### Connection Requests

Prioritize local HR leaders in target companies.

**Local-specific connection template:**

```
Hi [First Name], noticed you're in [City]. IAML is bringing our employment law training there in [Month]—thought it'd be good to connect with local HR leaders. Happy to be a resource.
```

### Content Strategy

Incorporate city-specific content into organic posting:

**Post types:**
- "Excited to bring IAML to [City] this [Month]..."
- "For HR professionals in [City]: we're hosting [Program] on [Dates]..."
- Feature testimonials from past attendees in that region (if available)

### Engagement Focus

Increase engagement with HR professionals in the target metro:
- Search for HR professionals in [City] who post actively
- Like and comment on their content
- Build visibility before/during campaign

## Virtual Cross-Sell Messaging

### Why Include Virtual

Every local campaign should mention virtual attendance:
- Captures prospects who can't attend in person
- Addresses objections (travel, time, approval)
- Demonstrates flexibility
- May convert to in-person for future programs

### When to Introduce

**Email 3 (Week 6-8)**: Primary virtual introduction
**All subsequent emails**: Mention virtual as alternative

### Virtual Messaging Framework

**Positioning**: Virtual as equal option, not lesser fallback

**Key messages:**
- "Same faculty, same content, same certificate"
- "Can't leave the office? Join us virtually"
- "Full Q&A capability, interactive sessions, recordings available"
- "Choose the format that works for your schedule"

## Tracking & Optimization

### Metrics to Track by City

| Metric | How to Track |
|--------|--------------|
| List size | Data compilation |
| Emails sent | Email platform |
| Open rate | Email platform |
| Click rate | Email platform |
| Registrations from local campaign | Supabase (source attribution) |
| In-person vs. virtual split | Supabase |
| Cost per registration | Supabase + cost tracking |

### Post-Campaign Analysis

After each local campaign:

1. **What worked?**
   - Which emails had highest open/click rates?
   - What subject lines performed best?
   - Did virtual messaging increase registrations?

2. **What didn't work?**
   - Which emails underperformed?
   - Were there timing issues?
   - List quality problems?

3. **City-specific learnings:**
   - Some cities may respond better than others
   - Note patterns for future campaigns

### Optimization Over Time

- **Template refinement**: Update templates based on performance
- **Timing adjustments**: Start earlier or later based on conversion patterns
- **List quality**: Improve data gathering for better targeting
- **Virtual balance**: Adjust virtual messaging based on uptake
