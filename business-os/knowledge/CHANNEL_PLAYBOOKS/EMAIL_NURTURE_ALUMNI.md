## Overview

### Warm Audiences

This playbook covers communication with people who already have a relationship with IAML:

| Audience | Relationship | Goal |
|----------|--------------|------|
| **Alumni** | Past participants | Re-engagement, repeat attendance, referrals |
| **Registered attendees** | Upcoming program | Preparation, excitement, show rate |
| **Engaged prospects** | Attended quarterly update or engaged with content | Conversion to paid programs |
| **Colleague referrals** | Referred by current/past participant | Capitalize on warm introduction |

### Key Principles

- **They know us**: Don't over-explain who IAML is
- **Respect the relationship**: Don't over-email; quality over quantity
- **Provide value**: Every email should offer something useful
- **Personal tone**: Warmer than cold outreach
- **Segmentation matters**: Different messages for different alumni segments

### Offer

### Campaign Structure

**Initial blast + 1 reminder, then transition to ongoing nurture**

Claim your access here: [link]

Looking forward to seeing you at an upcoming session.

## Colleague Referral Sequence

### Trigger

A participant registers for a program

### Goal

Encourage them to refer colleagues → colleagues register → both benefit from alumni/group pricing

### Domain

invitationtohrtraining.com

### Sequence Structure

**3 emails: Pre-program, during program, post-program**

---

### Email 1: Pre-Program Referral Prompt

**Timing**: 1 week after registration (before program)

**Subject lines:**
- Bringing a colleague to [Program Name]?
- Team training opportunity, [First Name]
- Invitation for your HR colleagues

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

We're looking forward to seeing you at [Program Name] on [dates] in [location/virtual].

Quick thought: would any of your colleagues benefit from attending with you?

When colleagues from the same organization attend together, you both gain:
• Shared language and frameworks for handling issues
• Ability to discuss real situations openly during the program
• Aligned approach when you return to work

Plus, colleagues you refer receive [discount amount] off their registration.

If someone comes to mind, just forward this email or have them contact us at [email/phone] and mention your name.

See you soon,

[Signature]
```

---

### Email 2: During Program (In-Person Only)

**Timing**: Day 2 of program

**Subject lines:**
- Thinking of anyone back at the office?
- Who else at [Company] should experience this?

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Hope you're finding the program valuable so far.

As you're going through the content, I'm wondering: is there anyone back at [Company] who would benefit from this training?

Many organizations send multiple team members over time to build consistent employment law expertise across their HR function.

If you'd like to refer a colleague, they'll receive [discount amount] off—and we'll extend the same to any future programs you attend.

Just reply with their name and email, or have them reach out directly and mention you.

Enjoy the rest of the program,

[Signature]
```

---

### Email 3: Post-Program Referral Prompt

**Timing**: 2 weeks after program completion

**Subject lines:**
- Know someone who needs this, [First Name]?
- Pass it on: IAML training
- Your colleagues could benefit too

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Now that you've completed [Program Name] and had a chance to apply what you learned, I have a question:

Is there a colleague who could benefit from the same training?

Many of our alumni find that sharing the IAML experience with colleagues creates:
• A common framework for handling employment law issues
• Backup when complex situations arise
• Stronger overall HR capability

When you refer a colleague:
• They receive [discount amount] off their registration
• You receive [discount amount] off your next IAML program

Just have them mention your name when they register, or reply to this email with their contact info.

Thanks for being part of the IAML community,

[Signature]
```

## Post-Quarterly Update Sequence

### Audience

Everyone who attends a quarterly update (alumni and prospects)

### Purpose

Keep them engaged, move prospects toward programs, remind alumni of benefits

---

### Email 1: Thank You + Recording

**Timing**: Day after quarterly update

**Subject lines:**
- [Topic] recording now available
- Thanks for joining us, [First Name]
- Session recording + resources

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Thanks for joining yesterday's Quarterly Employment Law Update on [Topic].

**Your recording is ready**: [link]

**Key takeaways from the session:**
• [Takeaway 1]
• [Takeaway 2]
• [Takeaway 3]

**Resources mentioned:**
• [Any resources, links, or materials referenced]

Our next quarterly update is [date] and covers [topic]. You'll receive a reminder as we get closer.

Questions about anything covered? Just reply.

[Signature]
```

---

### Email 2: Engagement Check-In (Prospects Only)

**Timing**: 5-7 days after quarterly update
**Audience**: Non-alumni attendees only

**Subject lines:**
- How did we do, [First Name]?
- Quick question about the session
- Thoughts on [Topic]?

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

I wanted to check in after last week's session on [Topic].

A few quick questions (reply is optional, but appreciated):

1. Did you find the session valuable?
2. Was there anything you wished we'd covered more deeply?
3. Would any of your colleagues benefit from future sessions?

If the quarterly update gave you a taste for what IAML offers, I'm happy to discuss our certificate programs—they're the deep dive version of what you experienced.

Either way, thanks for joining us. The next session is [date].

[Signature]
```
