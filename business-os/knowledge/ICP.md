## Market Overview

### Target Market Definition

IAML targets HR professionals, benefits specialists, and in-house legal counsel at companies with **250+ employees**, with an aspirational focus on **Fortune 500** companies. Our ideal customers are **proactive and preventative** in their approach to employment law education—they invest in training before problems occur, not in reaction to litigation or compliance failures.

### Market Context (2024-2025)

**HR Leadership Priorities:**
- 51% of CHROs identify leadership and manager development as top priority for 2025
- 61% cite wage inflation as a major challenge; 45% cite rising operational costs
- 75% believe managers are overwhelmed; 70% say current programs don't prepare managers for the future
- 57% of HR professionals report working beyond typical capacity; 56% are short-staffed

**Union Organizing Surge:**
- NLRB union election petitions increased 27% from FY2023 to FY2024 (3,286 petitions)
- Petitions have more than doubled since 2021
- Unions won 79% of elections in 2024 (up from 76% in 2023)
- Active organizing in healthcare, hospitality, manufacturing, retail, and tech
- High-risk industries: utilities (18.7% unionized), transportation/warehousing (15.8%), educational services (13.2%)

**Implications for IAML:**
- Strong demand for labor relations training (Comprehensive Labor Relations block)
- Companies facing organizing need immediate, practical guidance
- Preventative training is more valuable than ever given union win rates
- HR teams are stretched thin—training must be high-impact and immediately applicable

### Competitive Landscape Summary

| Competitor | Positioning | IAML Advantage |
|------------|-------------|----------------|
| SHRM | Brand recognition, scale, certification pathway | Depth of instruction, practicing attorneys, multi-day immersion |
| Law Firm CLEs | Free/low-cost, existing relationships | Comprehensive curriculum (not afternoon overview), multiple instructor perspectives, HR-focused (not lawyer-focused) |
| Internal Training | Convenient, company-specific | External expertise, current case law, networking with peers, certificate credential |

## Primary Segments

### Segment 1: HR Leadership

**Titles**: VP of HR, SVP of HR, CHRO, Chief People Officer

**Role Characteristics:**
- Strategic oversight of entire HR function
- Reports to CEO/COO or Board
- Budget authority for department training and professional development
- Responsible for organizational risk management related to people
- Often has legal/compliance accountability

**Psychographic Profile:**
- **Primary motivation**: Protecting the organization from legal exposure and liability
- **Secondary motivation**: Staying current on evolving employment law landscape
- **Career stage**: Established; focused on legacy and avoiding career-ending mistakes
- **Risk tolerance**: Low—they've seen what can go wrong
- **Decision style**: Deliberate, seeks validation from legal counsel and peers

**Pain Points:**
- Keeping up with rapidly changing employment law (NLRB decisions, state laws, ADA/FMLA updates)
- Ensuring their team has sufficient legal knowledge to handle day-to-day issues without escalating everything
- Union organizing threats, especially with increased NLRB activity
- AI integration concerns and workforce displacement decisions
- Building leadership pipeline while managing overwhelmed managers
- Balancing cost pressures with compliance requirements

**Content Triggers:**
- New NLRB rulings or guidance
- State-specific employment law changes (especially California, New York)
- High-profile employment lawsuits in their industry
- Union organizing at peer companies
- Regulatory changes (DOL, EEOC)

**IAML Programs (Personal Attendance):**
- Advanced Certificate in Strategic Employment Law
- Certificate in Employee Relations Law (especially Comprehensive Labor Relations block)

**IAML Programs (Team Development - Sends Others):**
- Certificate in Strategic HR Leadership (foundational program for developing HR talent)
- Certificate in Employee Relations Law
- Certificate in Workplace Investigations
- Certificate in Employee Benefits Law (for benefits staff)
- Advanced Certificate in Employee Benefits Law (for senior benefits staff)
- Corporate on-site training for management teams

**Relationship Building Entry Point:**
- Quarterly Employment Law Update (one-time complimentary access)
- Goal: Build relationship with HR leader, demonstrate IAML quality, open door to team development conversations

**Buying Behavior:**
- *To be validated*: Likely has discretionary budget for own development
- *To be validated*: Decision-maker for team training budgets
- *To be validated*: May require ROI justification for larger investments

**Engagement Strategy:**
- **Primary Entry Point**: One-time complimentary Quarterly Employment Law Update to demonstrate quality and begin relationship
- After attendance, follow up to build relationship and understand their team's development needs
- Executive-level content on LinkedIn (strategic, not tactical)
- Peer networking opportunities
- Corporate training proposals for their teams
- Position IAML as partner for developing their entire HR organization

**Intelligence to Gather:**
- [ ] Budget authority thresholds at different company sizes
- [ ] Who approves their personal training spend?
- [ ] Typical evaluation timeline for programs
- [ ] Which publications/sources do they trust?
- [ ] Do they attend conferences? Which ones?

### Segment 2: HR Director / Senior Manager

**Titles**: HR Director, Senior HR Manager, Director of Employee Relations, Director of HR Operations

**Role Characteristics:**
- Manages HR team or significant HR function
- Reports to VP/CHRO or may report to COO at smaller companies
- Handles complex employee relations issues directly
- Often the "go-to" person for difficult situations
- May have budget for team training; may need approval for personal development

**Psychographic Profile:**
- **Primary motivation**: Career advancement to VP/CHRO level
- **Secondary motivation**: Building expertise that makes them indispensable
- **Career stage**: Mid-career growth; ambitious but experienced
- **Risk tolerance**: Moderate—willing to advocate for resources if justified
- **Decision style**: Practical; looks for actionable takeaways

**Pain Points:**
- Handling complex employee situations without in-house legal support
- Staying current on employment law while managing daily operations
- Being seen as strategic partner vs. administrative function
- Developing team members with limited training budget
- Documenting decisions defensibly
- Managing investigations properly
- Navigating union organizing activity (if applicable)

**Content Triggers:**
- Specific case studies of employment law mistakes
- "How to" content for complex situations
- Career development and advancement content
- Certification/credential discussions
- Templates and decision frameworks

**IAML Programs (Primary Fit):**
- Certificate in Employee Relations Law (full program)
- Advanced Certificate in Strategic Employment Law
- Certificate in Workplace Investigations
- Certificate in Strategic HR Leadership

**Buying Behavior:**
- *To be validated*: May have small discretionary budget
- *To be validated*: Likely needs manager approval for $2,375 programs
- Often the person who researches and recommends training for themselves and team
- Price-conscious but understands value of quality

**Engagement Strategy:**
- Free block attendance (trial offer) to experience IAML quality
- Progression messaging: ERL → Advanced Certificate path
- Certificate credential positioning for career advancement
- LinkedIn content focused on practical application
- Case study content and scenario-based learning

**Intelligence to Gather:**
- [ ] What triggers the decision to seek training?
- [ ] Who approves their training requests?
- [ ] How far in advance do they plan for training?
- [ ] Do they have annual training budgets or ad hoc?
- [ ] What's their relationship with outside legal counsel?

### Segment 3: HR Manager / Generalist

**Titles**: HR Manager, HR Generalist, HR Business Partner, Employee Relations Manager

**Role Characteristics:**
- Handles broad range of HR responsibilities
- First line of defense for employee issues
- May be solo HR person at smaller companies or part of team at larger ones
- Directly interacts with employees and managers daily
- Often stretched thin across multiple responsibilities

**Psychographic Profile:**
- **Primary motivation**: Career advancement and building expertise
- **Secondary motivation**: Confidence handling situations correctly
- **Career stage**: Early to mid-career; building foundation
- **Risk tolerance**: Variable—often cautious due to inexperience
- **Decision style**: Seeks guidance and validation; appreciates frameworks

**Pain Points:**
- Lack of formal employment law training
- Uncertainty about handling specific situations correctly
- Limited access to legal counsel for questions
- Pressure to handle more with less support
- Keeping up with changing laws while managing daily workload
- Documentation and investigation skills gaps
- Fear of making costly mistakes

**Content Triggers:**
- "Am I handling this correctly?" scenarios
- Employment law basics and refreshers
- Templates, checklists, and guides
- Certification and career development
- Real-world case examples

**IAML Programs (Primary Fit):**
- Certificate in Employee Relations Law (foundational)
- Certificate in Strategic HR Leadership
- Certificate in Workplace Investigations
- Individual blocks for specific skill gaps

**Buying Behavior:**
- *To be validated*: Typically needs manager approval
- *To be validated*: May advocate strongly for training they believe in
- Price-sensitive; may start with blocks vs. full programs
- Often referred by senior HR leaders who attended IAML

**Engagement Strategy:**
- Free block attendance to experience quality
- Block → Full Program → Advanced progression
- Career advancement messaging
- CE credit emphasis (SHRM, HRCI)
- Practical, tactical LinkedIn content

**Intelligence to Gather:**
- [ ] What level of approval needed for training?
- [ ] Do they research training themselves or assigned by manager?
- [ ] What other training have they attended?
- [ ] How do they hear about training opportunities?
- [ ] What would make them advocate for IAML to their manager?

### Segment 4: Benefits Professional

**Titles**: Benefits Manager, Benefits Director, Compensation & Benefits Manager, VP of Total Rewards, Benefits Administrator (senior)

**Role Characteristics:**
- Specializes in employee benefits administration
- Navigates complex ERISA, ACA, and fiduciary requirements
- Works closely with brokers, vendors, and legal counsel
- Responsible for plan compliance and administration
- May oversee retirement plans, health benefits, or both

**Psychographic Profile:**
- **Primary motivation**: Ensuring compliance and avoiding costly penalties
- **Secondary motivation**: Optimizing benefits programs within budget constraints
- **Career stage**: Often specialized early; deep expertise track
- **Risk tolerance**: Very low—ERISA penalties are severe
- **Decision style**: Detail-oriented; regulatory-focused

**Pain Points:**
- ERISA compliance complexity
- ACA reporting and compliance
- Fiduciary liability concerns
- DOL and IRS audit preparation
- Health plan administration challenges
- Retirement plan compliance (401k, nondiscrimination testing)
- Managing benefits vendors and brokers
- Keeping up with regulatory changes

**Content Triggers:**
- DOL guidance changes
- IRS updates on retirement plans
- ACA reporting deadlines
- Fiduciary duty reminders
- ERISA litigation cases
- Benefits-specific regulatory updates

**IAML Programs (Primary Fit):**
- Certificate in Employee Benefits Law
- Advanced Certificate in Employee Benefits Law

**Buying Behavior:**
- *To be validated*: Often has dedicated benefits training budget
- *To be validated*: May be separate from HR training budget
- Highly values specialized, deep expertise
- Network of benefits professionals may share recommendations

**Engagement Strategy:**
- Benefits-specific quarterly updates
- Technical content on ERISA, ACA, fiduciary duties
- Separate nurture track from general HR content
- Partner with benefits consultants/brokers for referrals

**Intelligence to Gather:**
- [ ] Where do benefits professionals get information? (Different from general HR?)
- [ ] What associations/groups do they belong to?
- [ ] Do they attend benefits-specific conferences?
- [ ] How does their budget/approval process differ from HR?
- [ ] Are they typically part of HR or separate department?

### Segment 5: In-House Employment Counsel

**Titles**: Employment Counsel, Labor & Employment Attorney, Associate General Counsel (Employment), Senior Counsel

**Role Characteristics:**
- Licensed attorney specializing in employment law
- Advises HR on legal matters and risk management
- May handle employment litigation directly or manage outside counsel
- Reviews policies, terminations, and high-risk situations
- Often final decision-maker on legally sensitive matters

**Psychographic Profile:**
- **Primary motivation**: Staying current on evolving case law and regulations
- **Secondary motivation**: CLE credits and professional development
- **Career stage**: Varies; often experienced attorneys who moved in-house
- **Risk tolerance**: Very low—licensed professional with personal liability
- **Decision style**: Evidence-based; wants legal authority cited

**Pain Points:**
- Keeping current on rapidly changing employment law across jurisdictions
- Managing multi-state compliance complexity
- Training HR to handle routine matters without legal escalation
- Balancing business needs with legal risk
- CLE credit requirements
- NLRB and union-related legal developments

**Content Triggers:**
- New court decisions and case law
- NLRB rulings and guidance
- State law changes (especially California)
- EEOC guidance and enforcement trends
- Supreme Court employment decisions

**IAML Programs (Primary Fit):**
- Advanced Certificate in Strategic Employment Law
- Certificate in Employee Relations Law (full program or Comprehensive Labor Relations block)
- Certificate in Workplace Investigations

**IAML Programs (Team Referral):**
- May recommend HR team members attend HR-focused programs

**Buying Behavior:**
- *To be validated*: Often has separate professional development budget
- CLE credits are table stakes—won't attend without them
- Values depth and legal rigor over broad overview
- May compare to law firm CLE programs

**Engagement Strategy:**
- Emphasize CLE credit availability
- Position as complement to (not replacement for) law firm CLEs
- Highlight multiple instructor perspectives vs. single firm view
- Focus on practical application, not just legal theory
- Attorney-specific content track

**Intelligence to Gather:**
- [ ] What draws them to IAML vs. free law firm CLEs?
- [ ] How many CLE credits do they need annually?
- [ ] Do they recommend IAML to their HR colleagues?
- [ ] What legal publications/sources do they read?
- [ ] Are they active in any bar associations?

## Trigger-Based Segments

Beyond role-based segments, certain events create urgent training needs that cut across all roles.

### Union Organizing Activity

**Trigger Events:**
- Union card signing campaign begins
- NLRB election petition filed
- Increased union-related activity at peer companies
- Industry-wide organizing efforts (healthcare, hospitality, tech)
- Acquisition of unionized workforce

**Training Urgency**: HIGH—often need training within weeks

**Primary Programs:**
- Certificate in Employee Relations Law (Comprehensive Labor Relations block)
- Corporate on-site training for management teams

**Identification Methods:**
- NLRB petition filings (public record)
- News monitoring for organizing campaigns
- Industry association alerts
- LinkedIn signals (job postings for labor relations roles)

**Outreach Approach:**
- Direct outreach with specific, helpful content
- Avoid appearing opportunistic—focus on genuine help
- Offer rapid deployment of relevant training

---

### High-Regulation State Companies

**States of Focus:**
- California (complex employment law, frequent updates)
- New York (state and NYC requirements)
- Other states with unique requirements (Illinois, Massachusetts, etc.)

**Training Needs:**
- State-specific compliance training
- Multi-state employer considerations
- Staying current on frequent legislative changes

**Identification Methods:**
- Company headquarters or significant operations in target states
- Job postings indicating multi-state operations
- California-specific HR manager roles

---

### Recent Litigation or EEOC Activity

**Trigger Events:**
- EEOC charge filed
- Employment lawsuit
- DOL audit or investigation
- Settlement of employment claim

**Training Urgency**: MODERATE to HIGH

**Primary Programs:**
- Certificate in Workplace Investigations
- Certificate in Employee Relations Law
- Corporate training on specific issue areas

**Identification Methods:**
- EEOC conciliation announcements (public)
- Employment litigation filings (PACER)
- News coverage of settlements
- *Note: Approach with sensitivity—companies may be defensive*

---

### Newly Promoted HR Leaders

**Trigger Events:**
- Promotion to first VP/Director role
- New CHRO appointment
- HR leader joining from outside organization

**Training Urgency**: MODERATE—within first 6-12 months

**Training Needs:**
- Foundational employment law knowledge
- Strategic HR leadership skills
- Building credibility in new role

**Identification Methods:**
- LinkedIn job change alerts
- Company announcement monitoring
- Press releases on executive appointments

**Outreach Approach:**
- Congratulatory messaging
- Position training as support for new role success
- Peer success stories from similar transitions

---

### Growing Companies

**Trigger Events:**
- Funding round announced
- Rapid hiring (job posting volume increase)
- Geographic expansion
- Approaching employee count thresholds (50, 100, 250 employees)

**Training Urgency**: MODERATE

**Training Needs:**
- Building HR infrastructure
- Compliance with laws that apply at larger sizes
- Professionalizing HR function

**Identification Methods:**
- Funding announcements (Crunchbase, press)
- LinkedIn employee count growth
- Job posting monitoring
- Inc. 5000 and similar growth lists

## Buyer vs. Attendee Dynamics

Understanding who buys vs. who attends is critical for targeting and messaging.

### Scenario A: Self-Purchase

**Pattern**: Individual registers themselves for professional development

**Common For:**
- Entry to mid-level HR professionals
- Benefits specialists
- In-house counsel (CLE credits)

**Decision Factors:**
- Personal career development
- Specific skill gap to fill
- CE credit needs
- Price sensitivity may be higher

**IAML Approach:**
- Career advancement messaging
- ROI on personal investment
- Payment plan options if available
- Block attendance as entry point

---

### Scenario B: Team Builder (Manager Sends)

**Pattern**: Senior HR leader registers team members for development

**Common For:**
- HR Directors/VPs sending managers and generalists
- CHROs building team capability
- Benefits Directors developing staff

**Decision Factors:**
- Team skill gaps
- Risk mitigation (ensuring team can handle situations)
- Budget allocation
- Program quality and reputation

**IAML Approach:**
- Team development messaging
- Group discounts
- Progression paths for team
- Pre-program consultation value

---

### Scenario C: L&D/Training Department

**Pattern**: Corporate training function purchases for employees

**Common For:**
- Enterprise companies with formal L&D
- Companies with training budgets allocated to departments

**Decision Factors:**
- Vendor evaluation criteria
- CE credit availability
- Reporting and tracking capability
- Scalability

**IAML Approach:**
- Corporate training proposals
- LMS integration if applicable
- Formal ROI documentation
- Account management approach

## ABM Target Account Criteria

For the monthly 150 high-value target account selection:

### Tier 1 Criteria (Highest Priority)

**Must Have:**
- 1,000+ employees
- HR leadership titles identifiable on LinkedIn
- No IAML attendance in past 24 months

**Plus One or More:**
- Active union organizing (NLRB petition, news coverage)
- Headquarters or major operations in California
- Recent employment litigation (public records)
- Recent HR leadership change
- Industry with high unionization risk

### Tier 2 Criteria (High Priority)

**Must Have:**
- 500+ employees
- HR function identifiable

**Plus One or More:**
- Growth stage (funding, hiring surge)
- Multi-state operations
- Healthcare, hospitality, manufacturing, logistics industry
- Benefits complexity indicators

### Tier 3 Criteria (Standard Priority)

- 250-500 employees
- Growing companies
- Meets general target profile

### Account Research Protocol

For each target account, gather:
1. Company size and industry
2. HR leadership team (names, titles, LinkedIn)
3. Recent news (funding, litigation, organizing, leadership changes)
4. Geographic footprint
5. Union status if known
6. Any existing IAML history

### Outreach Sequence

1. **LinkedIn connection** with personalized note
2. **Complimentary quarterly update invitation**
3. **Free block attendance offer** (if engagement)
4. **Personalized program recommendation** (based on consultation)
5. **Corporate training conversation** (if multiple attendees)
