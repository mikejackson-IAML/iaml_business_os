## Brand Voice

### Voice Definition

**IAML speaks as a trusted expert who genuinely wants to help HR professionals succeed.**

We are confident in our expertise but never arrogant. We're direct and clear but never cold. We understand the real challenges HR professionals face because our faculty deals with them every day in courtrooms and boardrooms.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What It Means | What It's NOT |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| **Confident** | We know our expertise is unmatched. We state facts directly. | Not arrogant, not boastful |
| **Supportive** | We're partners in your success, from before the program through your entire career | Not distant, not transactional |
| **Practical** | We focus on what works in the real world, not theory | Not academic, not abstract |
| **Direct** | We say what we mean clearly and efficiently | Not salesy, not filled with jargon |
| **Trustworthy** | We've earned trust over 45 years with 80,000+ professionals | Not pushy, not making empty promises |

### Tone Spectrum

IAML's tone sits in the **professional-approachable** range—more casual than a law firm, more authoritative than a typical training company.

```
FORMAL ←――――――――――――――――――――――――――――――――――――→ CASUAL
         Law Firm    |  IAML  |    Generic Training
                     ↑
              We live here
```

**Tone adjustments by audience:**

| Audience | Tone Adjustment |
|----------|-----------------|
| In-House Counsel | Slightly more formal, legal terminology acceptable |
| Senior HR (VP+) | Strategic focus, peer-to-peer respect |
| Mid-Level HR | Encouraging, career-advancement oriented |
| HR Generalists | Supportive, confidence-building |
| Benefits Professionals | Technical precision, regulatory focus |

### The Emotional Undercurrent

Every piece of IAML communication should make the reader feel:

1. **Confident** — "I can handle this."
2. **Supported** — "IAML has my back, before, during, and after."
3. **Trusting** — "These people know what they're doing."

## Messaging Hierarchy

### Primary Message (The One Thing)

> **IAML provides comprehensive support before, during, and after your program. You're never alone in navigating employment law.**

This is the feeling we want every prospect and participant to have. It's not just a training program; it's an ongoing partnership.

### Supporting Messages (The Three Pillars)

**1. Practicing Attorneys, Not Academics**
> Your instructors handle real cases, defend real charges, and advise real employers—then teach you exactly what works.

**2. Deep Expertise, Not Surface Overview**
> Multi-day intensive programs give you comprehensive mastery, not a 90-minute overview you'll forget by Friday.

**3. Ongoing Support, Not One-and-Done**
> Pre-program consultation, quarterly updates, alumni resources, and faculty access keep you current and supported year-round.

**For website headers, elevator pitches, and opening statements:**

> "For 45 years, IAML has trained over 80,000 HR professionals through intensive programs taught by practicing employment attorneys. You'll gain the expertise to protect your organization and advance your career—with support that continues long after the program ends."

*Alternatives to test:*
- "Employment Law Expertise. Ongoing Support. Real Results."
- "Where HR Professionals Master Employment Law"
- "Practicing Attorneys. Practical Solutions. Professional Support."

## Testimonial Strategy

### How to Use Testimonials

**Testimonials are among IAML's strongest assets.** Use them strategically:

1. **Match testimonial to audience** — Use senior HR quotes for senior HR prospects, etc.
2. **Match testimonial to objection** — Address specific concerns with relevant quotes
3. **Include attribution** — Name, title, and company add credibility
4. **Keep them specific** — Specific outcomes beat generic praise

### Testimonial Categories

**Quality of Instruction:**
> "The Advanced Certificate in Strategic Employment Law is by far the best conference I have attended. I have never walked out of an IAML program without learning something that I could apply immediately."

**Practical Application:**
> "Felt like the presenter hit on all the important items that really related to what HR does."

**Instructor Excellence:**
> "Fantastic instructor! He was able to keep everyone engaged and the seminar interactive."

**Immediate Value:**
> "I really liked the presenter and it was definitely worth my time. It was interactive and gave me some really good skills and knowledge to take with me."

**Career Impact:**
> "If you can choose only one employment law conference, this is the one!"

**Ongoing Relationship:**
> "IAML once again surpassed my expectations."

### Testimonial Formatting

**Short format (for scattered use):**
> "I have never walked out of an IAML program without learning something that I could apply immediately."
> — *Senior HR Professional*

**Full format (for featured placement):**
> "The Advanced Certificate in Strategic Employment Law is by far the best conference I have attended. I have never walked out of an IAML program without learning something that I could apply immediately. If you can choose only one employment law conference, this is the one!"
>
> — **[Name]**, [Title], [Company]

## Objection Handling (Website & Marketing)

Since there are no sales conversations, the website and marketing materials must proactively address common objections.

### Objection: "It's expensive" / "Is it worth the investment?"

**Address with value framing:**

> "Your enrollment includes all live instruction, complete program materials, 29.75 continuing education credits, pre-program consultation, 12 months of quarterly employment law updates (4 sessions, $1,588 value), permanent access to living resources, and alumni benefits for all future programs. There are no hidden fees or additional charges."

> "Consider what a single employment lawsuit costs your organization—not just legal fees, but management time, morale impact, and potential settlement. One situation handled correctly because of what you learned at IAML pays for the program many times over."

**Testimonial support:**
> "I really liked the presenter and it was definitely worth my time."

---

### Objection: "I can't be away from the office that long"

**Address with value-of-time framing:**

> "4½ days of focused learning builds expertise you'll use for years. Compare that to months of webinars that add up to more time with less retention."

> "Can't leave the office? Join us virtually—same faculty, same content, same certificate."

**For individual blocks:**
> "Can't commit to the full program? Start with a single block on the topic most pressing for you. Many attendees complete their certificate over time."

---

### Objection: "Can't I just learn this online / through Google / with AI?"

**Address with risk framing:**

> "Employment law is incredibly nuanced—generic information can be dangerously misleading. When you're handling an ADA accommodation request or responding to a union campaign, the cost of getting it wrong is enormous."

> "You can't Google the judgment that comes from practicing attorneys sharing how they've handled similar situations, or the peer insights from HR professionals at other companies facing the same challenges."

---

### Objection: "Why IAML instead of SHRM?"

**Address with depth comparison:**

> "SHRM conferences offer broad HR exposure across many topics. IAML delivers deep expertise in employment law—4½ days focused entirely on one subject, taught by practicing attorneys who handle these issues daily."

> "Many of our attendees also attend SHRM events. The two serve different needs: breadth versus depth."

---

### Objection: "Our law firm provides free CLEs"

**Address with depth and perspective:**

> "Law firm CLEs are great for narrow topics in 2-3 hours. IAML provides comprehensive coverage—the full landscape of employment law—in a way that's not possible in an afternoon session. Plus, you get multiple attorney perspectives and peer insights from HR professionals at other organizations."

---

### Objection: "How current is the content?"

**Address with living-content positioning:**

> "Your materials are living resources that update continuously as employment law changes. Unlike static textbooks that become outdated immediately after publication, our content reflects current court decisions, regulatory changes, and compliance requirements."

> "Recent updates include the NLRB's Cemex decision, Stericycle handbook standards, post-Bostock LGBTQ+ protections, and state-specific AI hiring regulations."

## Calls to Action

### Primary CTAs (Test to optimize)

| CTA | Best For |
|-----|----------|
| **Register Now** | High-intent visitors, program pages |
| **View Schedule** | Mid-funnel, exploring options |
| **View Upcoming Programs** | Homepage, general awareness |
| **Get More Information** | Lower commitment, lead capture |
| **Download Program Details** | Content exchange, lead capture |
| **Let's Talk** | Corporate training, complex questions |
| **Reserve Your Spot** | Urgency, limited availability |

### CTA Placement Guidelines

- **Above the fold**: High-commitment CTA ("Register Now" or "View Schedule")
- **After value explanation**: Medium-commitment ("View Upcoming Programs")
- **After testimonials**: Reinforcement CTA ("Join 80,000+ HR Professionals")
- **Footer/sticky**: Persistent low-friction option ("Get More Information")

### CTA Language Tips

- Use action verbs: "Register," "View," "Download," "Reserve"
- Create specificity: "View March Programs" vs. generic "Learn More"
- Test urgency: "Reserve Your Spot" vs. "Register Now"
- Avoid: "Submit," "Click Here," "Buy Now"

## Story Framework

### The IAML Story Arc

Every piece of content should follow this arc:

**1. The Challenge** (Their pain)
> Employment law is complex, constantly changing, and the stakes are enormous. Most HR professionals never receive formal legal training—they learn through trial, error, and expensive mistakes.

**2. The Failed Alternatives** (What doesn't work)
> Conference sessions are too shallow. Law firm CLEs are too brief. Online resources are unreliable. Google and AI can give dangerously generic advice.

**3. The Solution** (IAML's approach)
> IAML provides intensive, multi-day programs taught by practicing employment attorneys who handle these exact issues daily—combined with ongoing support that continues long after the program ends.

**4. The Transformation** (What changes)
> You'll gain the expertise to handle complex situations confidently, the credential that demonstrates your commitment, and a support system that keeps you current as laws evolve.

**5. The Proof** (Why believe it)
> 45 years. 80,000+ professionals trained. Faculty from the nation's leading employment law firms. Testimonials from HR leaders who apply what they learned immediately.

### Page-Level Storytelling

**Program pages should follow this structure:**

1. **Hero**: Clear statement of transformation ("Master workplace law in 4½ days")
2. **Problem acknowledgment**: Brief nod to the challenge they face
3. **Solution overview**: What the program delivers
4. **Proof**: Credentials, testimonials, faculty
5. **Details**: Curriculum, format, logistics
6. **Objection handling**: FAQ section
7. **Final CTA**: Clear next step

## Channel-Specific Adaptations

### Website

- **Tone**: Confident, comprehensive, supportive
- **Length**: Full detail available for those who want it; scannable for those who don't
- **Story**: Full arc from challenge to transformation
- **CTAs**: Multiple throughout, escalating commitment

### Email (Cold Outreach)

- **Tone**: Direct, respectful, value-first
- **Length**: Short—one clear point, one clear ask
- **Story**: Abbreviated—focus on one pain point and IAML's solution
- **CTAs**: Single, clear action

**Example structure:**
> [Personalization / Context]
> [One pain point they likely have]
> [How IAML addresses it]
> [Single CTA]

### Email (Nurture / Alumni)

- **Tone**: Warm, familiar, supportive
- **Length**: Medium—enough to provide value
- **Story**: Continuation of relationship, ongoing value
- **CTAs**: Soft (engage with content) or hard (register for program) depending on purpose

### LinkedIn (Organic)

- **Tone**: Thought leadership, peer-to-peer, conversational
- **Length**: Varies—short posts for engagement, longer for depth
- **Story**: Insight-driven, not promotional
- **CTAs**: Engagement-focused (comment, share) or soft program mentions

**Post types:**
- Employment law updates and insights
- Faculty perspectives on current issues
- Testimonial highlights
- Program announcements (sparingly)
- Industry trends and research

### LinkedIn (Outreach/DM)

- **Tone**: Personal, specific, not templated
- **Length**: Very short—2-3 sentences max
- **Story**: Connection-focused, value-offer
- **CTAs**: Single, low-friction (accept connection, reply, attend free session)
