Why this program

Small first responses can create big HR and legal consequences.

Employment law issues rarely arrive labeled as legal issues. They show up as performance concerns, attendance problems, uncomfortable conversations, informal complaints, scheduling decisions, workplace conflict, or quick questions from employees.

Quick answer

This 4-hour live virtual or on-site IAML program is built for frontline managers, supervisors, department heads, and operations leaders. It teaches practical first-response habits for complaints, documentation, discipline, retaliation, harassment, discrimination, leave/accommodation signals, and when to involve HR.

Earlier
Recognize risk before the pattern is set.

Managers learn to notice complaint, retaliation, harassment, discrimination, leave, accommodation, and documentation signals earlier.

Cleaner
Document facts instead of reactions.

Participants learn how vague, emotional, inconsistent, or after-the-fact notes can create avoidable HR problems.

Smarter
Know when to pause and involve HR.

The program gives managers practical escalation habits so they do not improvise through sensitive employee issues.

Curriculum / training path

Practical issue spotting for the workplace decisions managers actually make.

The training is organized around common manager moments: an employee raises a concern, performance slips, discipline is considered, leave or accommodation signals appear, or conflict needs a careful first response.

Who should attend

For leaders whose everyday decisions affect employee relations risk.

The program is designed for people managers and supervisors who need practical guidance, not legal theory, on how to handle workplace issues consistently and when to involve HR.

Lead

Support

FAQ

Questions managers, supervisors, HR partners, and operations leaders ask before sending people.

Get clear answers on audience fit, manager readiness, workplace topics, group attendance, and how this training should be used alongside HR and legal guidance.

Is this program for HR professionals or managers?

The program is designed primarily for frontline managers and supervisors. HR partners may attend or observe when they are supporting a manager group, but the training is written for the people who hear employee concerns first and make day-to-day workplace decisions.

Is this a certificate program?

Participants can receive an IAML certificate of completion documenting the 4-hour manager and supervisor training. This is a practical completion credential for internal development records, not a full employment-law certificate program for HR professionals.

Does this program offer SHRM, HRCI, or CLE credit?

Credit eligibility should be confirmed with IAML before registration. The program is designed first for frontline managers and supervisors, not as an HR certification course. HR professionals who attend may be able to use the completion documentation for applicable self-reported professional development where their credential rules allow HR-related training.

Do managers need legal knowledge to attend?

No. The program helps managers recognize risk, respond appropriately, and know when to involve HR. They do not need to become employment lawyers.

What workplace issues does the program cover?

Topics include complaint response, harassment and discrimination basics, retaliation risk, documentation, discipline, performance conversations, leave and accommodation issue spotting, investigation boundaries, and HR escalation.

Can a company send one manager or a larger group?

Yes. Organizations can send one manager to the live virtual program, reserve seats for multiple managers, or ask about on-site training for a larger private group. Request program details and IAML can help identify the best fit.

Is this a substitute for legal advice?

No. This is practical educational training. It helps managers recognize workplace issues and know when to involve HR or legal counsel, but it does not replace legal advice for a specific situation.

Can HR leaders observe?

Yes. HR leaders may observe at no additional cost when their organization sends managers to the live virtual session.
